## What NOCs Do You Need?

NOC (No Objection Certificate) is a declaration from a specific authority or party stating they have no objection to the property transaction. Different property types require different NOCs.

## NOC Master List

### For Apartment/Flat Purchase

| NOC | From Whom | When Required |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Society/Association NOC | Housing society | Resale — always required |
| Builder NOC | Developer | If society not formed yet |
| Bank NOC | Seller's bank | If property is mortgaged |
| Income Tax NOC | IT Department | For properties above ₹50 L (Form 34A) |

### For Plot/Land Purchase

| NOC | From Whom | When Required |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Agricultural land NOC | District Collector | If land is agricultural |
| Gram Panchayat NOC | Panchayat | For rural/semi-urban land |
| Urban Land Ceiling NOC | State government | In applicable states |
| Layout Authority NOC | BDA/DTCP/Development Authority | For plotted development |

### For Builder/New Project

| NOC | From Whom | When Required |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Fire Department NOC | Fire Service | Multi-storey buildings |
| Environmental NOC | SEIAA/MOEF | Large projects |
| Airport Authority NOC | AAI | Properties near airports |
| Water Supply/Sewage NOC | Municipal body | All new constructions |
| Electricity Board NOC | DISCOM | For power connection |

## Most Important NOCs for Buyers

### 1. Society/Association NOC (Resale)
Without society NOC, the transfer cannot happen. The society must approve the incoming buyer. Some societies charge a transfer fee (typically ₹25,000-₹5,00,000 depending on city and society).

### 2. Bank NOC (Mortgaged Property)
If the seller has an active home loan, the bank must issue a NOC confirming the outstanding amount and agreeing to release the mortgage after payment.

### 3. Income Tax Department NOC
For properties above threshold values, Form 34A must be submitted. The IT Department verifies there are no tax claims on the property.

## How to Obtain Key NOCs

| NOC | Process | Timeline |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Society NOC | Apply to society secretary with sale agreement | 15-30 days |
| Bank NOC | Seller requests from their bank | 7-15 days |
| Fire NOC | Builder applies with fire safety plans | 30-60 days |
| Environmental NOC | Builder applies to SEIAA | 60-120 days |

## Red Flags to Watch For

1. **Society refuses NOC** — may indicate pending disputes or objections
2. **Builder cannot produce fire/environmental NOC** — project may not be compliant
3. **Bank NOC has conditions** — read carefully for any restrictions
4. **No agricultural NOC for converted land** — conversion may be incomplete

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can a society refuse NOC for transfer?

A society can refuse NOC only on valid grounds (pending dues, violation of bye-laws). Arbitrary refusal is challengable under cooperative society laws. Get legal advice if NOC is denied.

### What if seller's bank doesn't give NOC?

This usually means the seller has not cleared outstanding dues. The bank NOC process can be done at the sub-registrar office simultaneously — buyer's bank pays seller's bank directly.
