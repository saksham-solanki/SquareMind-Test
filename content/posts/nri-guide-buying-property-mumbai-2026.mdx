## Why NRIs Are Investing in Mumbai in 2026

Mumbai continues to attract significant NRI investment due to its status as India's financial capital. The combination of coastal metro expansion, new infrastructure (Mumbai Trans Harbour Link, Metro Line 3), and RERA-compliant projects makes it a top destination for NRI property buyers.

This guide covers everything specific to Mumbai — from the best areas and builders to Maharashtra-specific stamp duty rules and registration processes.

## Best Areas in Mumbai for NRI Property Investment

### Premium Localities

The most popular areas for NRI investment in Mumbai include South Mumbai, Worli, Bandra West, and Powai. These localities offer strong rental yields (2.5-3.5%), good appreciation potential, and proximity to major business districts like BKC.

### Emerging Investment Zones

For NRIs looking at higher appreciation potential with moderate entry prices, consider Andheri East, Thane, and Navi Mumbai. These areas benefit from upcoming metro lines and offer 30-50% lower prices compared to established localities.

## Maharashtra Stamp Duty and Registration for NRI Buyers

Maharashtra Stamp Act mandates 6% stamp duty in Mumbai (5% for women buyers as sole or first-named purchaser). Registration charges are 1% additional, capped at Rs 30,000. Metro cess of 1% applies in Mumbai Municipal Corporation limits.

**Important for NRIs:** Stamp duty must be paid from NRO/NRE account or through a direct foreign remittance. Cash payments are not permitted and create compliance issues.

## Top Builders in Mumbai for NRI Buyers

Reputable developers with dedicated NRI sales desks in Mumbai include Lodha Group, Godrej Properties, Oberoi Realty, and Hiranandani. When evaluating builders:

1. RERA registration number on MahaRERA portal (maharera.mahaonline.gov.in)
2. Builder's delivery track record across previous Mumbai projects
3. Dedicated NRI support team for documentation and POA assistance
4. Bank tie-ups for NRI home loans with SBI, HDFC, ICICI

## FEMA Compliance for Mumbai Property

All standard [FEMA rules for NRI property investment](/insights/nri-buying-property-india-guide-2026) apply. Key Mumbai-specific points:

- Purchase payments must come from NRE/NRO account or direct foreign remittance
- Agricultural/gaothan land in Mumbai suburbs requires RBI approval
- POA must be executed at the Indian Consulate in your country of residence
- Documents must be attested per Maharashtra Sub-Registrar requirements

## NRI Tax Implications for Mumbai Property

Use our [NRI Tax Calculator](/tools/nri-tax-calculator) to estimate your tax liability on rental income and capital gains from Mumbai property.

### Rental Income

Rental income from Mumbai property is taxable in India at 30% TDS (deducted by tenant). File Indian ITR to claim deductions (municipal taxes, 30% standard deduction, home loan interest) and get refunds if effective rate is lower.

### Capital Gains on Sale

LTCG at 12.5% without indexation (post Budget 2024). TDS at 20% deducted by buyer on full sale consideration. Apply for lower TDS certificate under Section 197 before sale completion.

## Property Verification for Remote NRI Buyers

1. **Title search report** from an independent lawyer (not builder-recommended)
2. **Encumbrance certificate** for 30 years from Maharashtra Sub-Registrar
3. **MahaRERA registration** verified on the official portal
4. **IOD and CC** (Commencement Certificate) from BMC
5. **Occupation Certificate** for ready-to-move properties
