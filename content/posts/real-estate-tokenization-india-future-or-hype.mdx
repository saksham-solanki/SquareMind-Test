## Tokenization: The Promise and the Reality

Real estate tokenization — dividing property into blockchain-based digital tokens that can be bought and sold — promises to democratize property investment. In India, the reality is more complicated.

## How Tokenization Works

| Step | Process | Current Status in India |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Property valued and audited | Standard practice |
| 2 | Legal entity (SPV) created to hold property | Legal, common in fractional |
| 3 | Property divided into tokens | Technology exists |
| 4 | Tokens sold to investors | Limited platforms |
| 5 | Rental income distributed to token holders | Functional in pilot projects |
| 6 | Tokens traded on secondary market | Very limited liquidity |

## Tokenization vs Existing Options

| Feature | Direct Ownership | REITs | Fractional (non-token) | Tokenization |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Minimum investment | Rs 20L+ | Rs 300-500 | Rs 10-25L | Rs 5,000-50,000 |
| Liquidity | Low (months to sell) | High (stock exchange) | Low (platform-dependent) | Theoretical high |
| Regulation | RERA | SEBI | SEBI (proposed) | Unclear |
| Income distribution | Direct rent | Quarterly dividend | Monthly/quarterly | Smart contract |
| Capital gains | 20% LTCG | Stock exchange rules | Unclear | Unclear |
| Track record | Centuries | 5+ years in India | 3-5 years | Pilot stage |

## Regulatory Status in India

As of 2026, real estate tokenization exists in a regulatory grey area:

- **No specific SEBI framework** for tokenized real estate
- **RBI caution** on crypto/blockchain financial instruments
- **Fractional ownership** (without tokens) is being formalized under SEBI's SM REIT framework
- **Tax treatment** of tokenized real estate income is undefined

**Bottom line:** Tokenization technology works, but the regulatory and tax framework doesn't support it yet in India.

## Should You Invest?

### Wait if:
- You want regulatory clarity (smart decision for most investors)
- You need tax-efficient investing (tokenization tax treatment is undefined)
- You prioritize liquidity (secondary markets for tokens don't exist meaningfully)
- You need track record data (too early for performance evaluation)

### Consider if:
- You want commercial real estate exposure with Rs 10-25L budget
- You understand and accept platform risk
- You're comfortable with 3-5 year lock-in periods
- You've verified the platform's legal structure and RERA compliance

## The Honest Assessment

Real estate tokenization will eventually become mainstream in India — but "eventually" means 5-10 years, not 1-2 years. The current options are closer to fractional ownership than true tokenization. For most investors, REITs (Rs 300-500 minimum, stock exchange listed, SEBI regulated) offer a better risk-adjusted path to real estate diversification.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is real estate tokenization legal in India?

There's no law specifically banning it, but no regulatory framework supports it either. Fractional ownership (without blockchain) is being formalized under SEBI's SM REIT framework. True blockchain tokenization awaits regulatory clarity.

### Are fractional ownership returns guaranteed?

No. Returns depend on rental income and property appreciation — same risks as direct ownership. Platform fees (1-2% annually) reduce net returns. Verify the underlying property's rental history and location fundamentals.

### Should I invest in REITs instead?

For most retail investors, SEBI-regulated REITs (Embassy, Mindspace, Brookfield) offer better liquidity, regulatory protection, and diversification than fractional platforms. Start with REITs unless you have specific reasons to prefer fractional.
