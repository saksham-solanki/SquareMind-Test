## Can NRIs Own Property Jointly with Residents?

Yes. An NRI can jointly own residential or commercial property with an Indian resident. The most common scenario is NRI spouse + resident spouse, or NRI child + resident parent. FEMA permits this without any restrictions for residential and commercial property.

## Types of Joint Ownership

### Joint Tenancy

Both owners have equal, undivided interest. On death of one owner, the property automatically passes to the surviving owner (right of survivorship). Not common in India.

### Tenancy in Common

Owners can have unequal shares (60-40, 70-30, etc.). Each owner can transfer their share independently. More common and flexible for NRI-resident combinations.

## FEMA Rules for Joint Ownership

| Combination | Permitted? | Conditions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| NRI + Resident Indian | Yes | NRI's share must be paid from NRE/NRO/foreign remittance |
| NRI + NRI | Yes | Both must pay through approved channels |
| NRI + Foreign National | No | Foreign nationals cannot buy Indian property |
| NRI + OCI holder | Yes | OCI follows same rules as NRI |

## Tax Benefits of Joint Ownership

### Stamp Duty

If one co-owner is a woman (wife, mother, daughter), many states offer 1-2% stamp duty concession. Making the woman the first-named purchaser maximizes this benefit.

### Home Loan

Both co-owners can take a joint home loan and claim separate tax deductions:

- Section 80C: Up to Rs 1.5 lakh each (principal repayment)
- Section 24(b): Up to Rs 2 lakh each (interest on home loan)
- Combined benefit: Up to Rs 7 lakh annually

### Rental Income

Split rental income proportionally between co-owners. Each files their own ITR. If one co-owner is in a lower tax bracket, effective tax rate on rental income reduces.

## Documentation Requirements

1. Sale deed must clearly state each owner's share percentage
2. NRI's payment must be traceable to NRE/NRO account
3. Resident's payment from their savings/loan account
4. Separate PAN cards for both owners
5. Joint home loan agreement (if applicable)
