## Goa: Different Market, Different Rules

Goa's real estate market is fundamentally different from metro cities. It is driven by vacation homes, retirement living, and short-term rental income rather than IT sector employment. Builder dynamics are unique.

## Goa-Specific Risks

1. **CRZ (Coastal Regulation Zone)** — many Goa properties fall under CRZ restrictions. Verify CRZ clearance before buying.
2. **Land title complexity** — Portuguese-era land records create title verification challenges unique to Goa.
3. **Communidade land** — community-owned land with complex transfer rules. Avoid unless legal counsel confirms clear title.
4. **TCP (Town and Country Planning) approval** — separate from RERA. Verify TCP permission for construction.

## Our Recommendation

In Goa, stick to national brands (Tata, Godrej) for safety, or thoroughly verify local developer credentials. **Never skip legal due diligence in Goa** — the land title risks are higher than any other state.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is buying property in Goa a good investment?

Goa property offers rental yield potential (4-6% from vacation rentals) but appreciation is location-dependent. North Goa (Calangute-Anjuna belt) has peaked; South Goa and inland locations offer better value.

### Can non-Goans buy property in Goa?

Yes, Indian citizens can buy property in Goa. There are no restrictions for non-Goan Indian citizens. NRIs follow standard NRI purchase rules. Portuguese passport holders have additional considerations.
