## Chennai: The Quiet Outperformer of Indian Real Estate

Chennai doesn't generate the same headlines as Bangalore or Hyderabad, but it has quietly delivered 10-14% annual appreciation in IT corridors since 2021. The city's real estate advantage is stability — diversified employment across IT, manufacturing, automotive, and healthcare means demand doesn't swing with any single sector.

Chennai also has something that Bangalore desperately lacks: water. Desalination plants and reservoir management have reduced water risk, making it a more sustainable long-term real estate bet.

## OMR (Siruseri-Kelambakkam): Our Top Pick

The southern OMR stretch from Siruseri to Kelambakkam is Chennai's strongest investment corridor in 2026:

- **SIPCOT IT Park** and Tidel Park Siruseri house Cognizant, Infosys, and HCL campuses
- **Prices at ₹4,500-6,500/sqft** are 30-40% below northern OMR (Sholinganallur)
- **Metro Phase 2 extension** confirmed to reach Siruseri by 2028
- **Builder quality:** Prestige, Casagrand, and Radiance have active projects

**Risk:** Last-mile connectivity from OMR to the IT parks can be congested. Buy within 2 km of major junctions.

### Porur-Mount Poonamallee: The Western Alternative

Porur benefits from proximity to DLF IT Park, RMZ Millennia, and the upcoming Porur-Maduravoyal elevated corridor. At ₹5,500-7,500/sqft, it offers an alternative to OMR with better established social infrastructure.

## The Bottom Line

Chennai offers India's most stable real estate investment environment — diversified employment, adequate water, improving metro connectivity, and consistent 10-14% appreciation in IT corridors. The southern OMR stretch (Siruseri-Kelambakkam) is the standout pick for 2026, combining affordable entry with strong IT demand and metro catalyst.
