## Goa: India's Most Complex Land Title Market

Goa has the most complex property verification requirements in India. Portuguese-era land records, Comunidade (community) land, CRZ restrictions, and evolving TCP rules create a minefield for unprepared buyers.

## Step 1: Goa RERA Verification

Check rera.goa.gov.in for project registration. Relatively new authority — enforcement is developing.

Use our [RERA Verification Tool](/tools/rera-verifier) for verification.

## Step 2: Land Title Investigation (Critical)

Goa land records have unique complexities:

**Form I & XIV** — Key revenue documents. Form I shows ownership. Form XIV shows survey details and boundaries.
- Obtain from Mamlatdar office
- Verify seller's name matches Form I

**Comunidade Land** — Community-owned land managed by village associations. This land **cannot be sold** — only leased.
- Check with the Comunidade office whether the land is classified as Comunidade
- If yes, only lease is possible (typically 99 years)
- Verify lease terms and renewal conditions

**Mundkar (Tenant) Rights** — Under the Mundkar Act, long-standing tenants on land have rights that cannot be easily revoked.
- Check if any mundkar claims exist on the property
- Get a mundkar-free certificate from the Mamlatdar

## Step 3: CRZ (Coastal Regulation Zone) Clearance

Goa's coastline is heavily regulated under CRZ norms.

| CRZ Zone | Restriction |
| --- | --- |
| CRZ-I | No construction allowed (ecologically sensitive) |
| CRZ-II | Development allowed per existing norms |
| CRZ-III | No construction within 200m of High Tide Line (rural) |
| CRZ-IV | Water areas — no construction |

**Verify:** CRZ status at Goa Coastal Zone Management Authority (GCZMA). Many beach-proximity properties violate CRZ norms.

## Step 4: TCP (Town and Country Planning) Permission

TCP permission is separate from RERA and is mandatory for any construction in Goa.

- Verify Regional Plan zoning — is the land in a Settlement Zone?
- Check for Eco-Sensitive Zone restrictions (especially near wildlife sanctuaries)
- Building permission from TCP department
- Sanad (construction permission certificate)

## Red Flags to Watch For

1. **Comunidade land sold as freehold** — illegal; the sale is void
2. **CRZ violation** — property may face demolition orders
3. **No TCP permission** — construction is illegal without TCP approval
4. **Mundkar claims** — tenant rights that survive property sale
5. **Portuguese-era succession disputes** — multiple claimants under Portuguese civil code
6. **Power of Attorney sales** — extremely risky in Goa; insist on direct sale deed

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I buy Comunidade land in Goa?

No. Comunidade land can only be leased, not sold. Any "sale deed" for Comunidade land is legally void. Verify land classification before any payment.

### How do I check CRZ status of a Goa property?

Request CRZ NOC from the Goa Coastal Zone Management Authority. Alternatively, check the approved CRZ map for the village/taluka. Any property within 200m of the High Tide Line in CRZ-III areas cannot have new construction.
