## Delhi NCR: India's Most Complex Real Estate Market

Delhi NCR spans four states (Delhi, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan) and multiple regulatory environments. This complexity creates both risk and opportunity — prices can vary 300% between locations 20 km apart, and regulatory arbitrage between states creates unique investment windows.

The transformative infrastructure of 2026: Dwarka Expressway completion, Jewar Airport progress, Delhi Metro Phase 4, and the Delhi-Mumbai Expressway are reshaping the investment map.

## Dwarka Expressway: The Headline Story

The Dwarka Expressway (NPR) is now fully operational, connecting Dwarka to Gurgaon in 20 minutes. This single infrastructure event has driven 40-60% price appreciation in adjacent sectors over 3 years. Key players: DLF, Godrej, Sobha, Adani (M2K), and Tata Housing all have major projects.

**Current state:** Prices have already run significantly. At ₹8,000-14,000/sqft, the easy money has been made. We rate it A- for new investment — still good but not the 2022-era entry point.

**Best pockets:** Sector 113 (DLF projects, premium), Sector 37D (value segment), Sector 108 (balanced).

### Greater Noida West: The Value Play

Greater Noida West (Gaur City area) at ₹3,500-5,500/sqft offers NCR's most affordable entry from known builders (Gaur, ATS, Ace). The Jewar Airport effect and Noida Metro extension are long-term catalysts.

**Risk:** Builder delivery track record in Noida/Greater Noida is NCR's worst. Stick to delivered or near-delivered projects only.
