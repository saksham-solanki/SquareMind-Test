## Stop Investing on Gut Feeling — Use Data

Most Indian property investors rely on broker recommendations, newspaper ads, and word-of-mouth. The result? Overpaying by 10–20% and missing better opportunities in adjacent micro-markets.

In 2026, market data is freely available — you just need to know what to look at and where to find it.

## The 5 Market Indicators That Matter

### 1. Price Per Square Foot Trend (3-Year)

Don't look at absolute price — look at the trend. A locality with ₹6,000/sqft growing at 15% CAGR will outperform a ₹12,000/sqft locality growing at 5%.

| Growth Category | CAGR Range | What It Means |
| --- | --- | --- |
| High Growth | 12%+ | Emerging corridor, infrastructure-driven |
| Moderate Growth | 6–12% | Established area, steady appreciation |
| Stagnant | 0–6% | Mature market, limited upside |
| Declining | Negative | Oversupply or infrastructure issues |

### 2. Rental Yield (Gross and Net)

Gross rental yield = (Annual Rent / Property Price) x 100. Net yield subtracts maintenance, vacancy, and taxes.

Indian real estate typically yields 2–5% gross. Anything above 4% is excellent. Use our [Rental Yield Calculator](/tools/rental-yield) for precise calculations.

### 3. Supply Pipeline

Check how many new units are launching in the locality. Oversupply kills both rental yield and appreciation. Data sources:
- RERA portal project registrations
- Builder launch announcements
- Knight Frank / JLL quarterly reports

### 4. Absorption Rate

How quickly are new units selling? A healthy market absorbs 70%+ of launched inventory within 6 months. Below 50% indicates weak demand or overpricing.

### 5. Infrastructure Timeline

Announced infrastructure (metro, expressway, airport) drives speculation. But only confirmed, under-construction projects with visible progress should influence your decision.

## Where to Find Reliable Data

| Source | What It Provides | Reliability |
| --- | --- | --- |
| State RERA Portal | Project registrations, delivery status | High |
| NHB RESIDEX | City-level price indices | High |
| Knight Frank Reports | Quarterly market analysis | High |
| 99acres / Magicbricks | Listing prices (not transaction) | Medium |
| Registrar Data (IGR) | Actual transaction prices | High |
| Local Brokers | Micro-market intelligence | Variable |

## FAQs

### How often should I check market data?

Quarterly for trends, monthly if actively buying. Real estate data doesn't change as fast as stock markets.

### Can I trust listing prices on portals?

No — listing prices are typically 5–15% higher than actual transaction prices. Always verify with registrar data or recent transactions in the same building.

### Is there a free source for transaction data?

Some states publish IGR (Inspector General of Registration) data online. Maharashtra's IGR portal is the most comprehensive.
