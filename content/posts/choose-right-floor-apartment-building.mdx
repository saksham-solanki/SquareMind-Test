## Floor Selection: More Important Than You Think

The floor you choose affects price, natural light, noise, safety, and resale value. Most first-time buyers do not think about this carefully enough.

## Floor-Wise Comparison

| Factor | Ground/1st Floor | Mid Floors (3-7) | Higher Floors (8+) | Top Floor |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Price premium | Base or discount | +2-5% | +5-10% | +8-15% or discount |
| Natural light | Limited | Good | Excellent | Best |
| Noise from road | High | Moderate | Low | Very low |
| Mosquitoes/insects | High | Moderate | Low | Low |
| View | None/limited | Partial | Good | Best |
| Lift dependency | None | Moderate | High | Highest |
| Safety (flood/earthquake) | Flood risk | Moderate | Higher risk | Highest risk |
| Resale | Average | Best | Good | Variable |
| Elderly-friendly | Yes | With lift | Lift-dependent | Lift-dependent |

## Price Premium by Floor (Typical)

| Floor | Premium vs Base |
| --- | --- |
| Ground floor | -5% to 0% |
| 1st-2nd floor | Base price |
| 3rd-5th floor | +2-4% |
| 6th-10th floor | +4-8% |
| 11th-15th floor | +6-10% |
| 16th+ floor | +8-12% |
| Top floor (terrace) | +10-15% or -5% (waterproofing concerns) |

## Best Floor Recommendations

| If You Prioritize | Best Floor |
| --- | --- |
| Children safety + garden | Ground or 1st |
| Elderly parents living with you | 1st-3rd (manageable without lift) |
| Working professional (view, quiet) | 5th-10th |
| Investment (resale value) | 3rd-7th |
| Premium lifestyle | 10th+ |
| Budget | Ground or top floor |

## Top Floor: Special Considerations

Pros: best view, most privacy, often terrace access. Cons: waterproofing issues (especially in older buildings), highest heat in summer, most lift-dependent.

**Check:** Quality of waterproofing, recent leakage history, AC load capacity, water pressure.
