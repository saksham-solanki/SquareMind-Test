## OC vs CC: Why Buyers Get Confused

Occupancy Certificate (OC) and Completion Certificate (CC) are different documents issued by different authorities for different purposes. Confusing them can cost you dearly.

## Completion Certificate (CC)

**What it is:** A certificate from the local planning authority confirming that the building was constructed according to approved plans.

**Issued by:** Municipal corporation or development authority (BMC, BBMP, GHMC, PMC, etc.)

**Confirms:**
- Building matches approved plans (height, FSI, setbacks)
- Structural safety standards met
- Fire safety norms complied with

**When issued:** After construction completion but before occupation

## Occupancy Certificate (OC)

**What it is:** A certificate from the local authority permitting people to occupy the building.

**Issued by:** Same authority that issues CC, but after additional inspections

**Confirms everything in CC, plus:**
- Water supply connected
- Sewage system functional
- Electricity connection in place
- Lift safety certificate obtained
- Fire department NOC received
- Building is safe for habitation

**When issued:** After CC, once all utilities are connected

## Why You Need Both

| Document | What Happens Without It |
| --- | --- |
| No CC | Building may violate approved plans — demolition risk |
| No OC | Cannot legally occupy — utility connections may be illegal |

## Risks of Buying Without OC

1. **Legal occupation risk** — you're living in a building not certified for habitation
2. **Utility disconnection** — electricity and water can be disconnected if OC is not obtained
3. **Bank loan issues** — banks may not finance properties without OC
4. **Resale difficulty** — future buyers will face the same issues
5. **Insurance problems** — claims may be denied for unoccupied buildings

## How to Verify OC/CC

1. Ask builder for certified copies of OC and CC
2. Verify at the municipal corporation or development authority
3. Check that OC covers your specific wing/tower/phase
4. Verify OC date — should be before your proposed possession date

## Red Flags to Watch For

1. **Builder offering possession without OC** — illegal; don't accept keys
2. **Partial OC** — verify your specific unit is covered
3. **CC issued but OC pending for years** — utility compliance issues
4. **OC copy not available for inspection** — builder may not have obtained it

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I take possession without OC?

Legally, no. RERA mandates that builders must obtain OC before giving possession. However, some builders offer "possession" without OC — this is a violation. Do not accept.

### What if my builder delays OC?

File a complaint with RERA. You are entitled to compensation for delayed possession, including interest on the amount paid. The builder cannot charge you for possession delays caused by OC delays.
