## Investing on a Salary: The Numbers-First Approach

Salaried professionals have one advantage over business owners: predictable income. This makes EMI planning straightforward. The challenge is matching your salary bracket to the right city and micro-market.

| Monthly Salary (CTC) | Max EMI (40% rule) | Loan Amount (20yr, 8.5%) | Property Budget (80% LTV) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Rs 50,000 | Rs 20,000 | Rs 22L | Rs 28L |
| Rs 75,000 | Rs 30,000 | Rs 33L | Rs 41L |
| Rs 1,00,000 | Rs 40,000 | Rs 44L | Rs 55L |
| Rs 1,50,000 | Rs 60,000 | Rs 66L | Rs 83L |
| Rs 2,00,000 | Rs 80,000 | Rs 88L | Rs 1.1 Cr |

**Rule:** Keep EMI below 40% of take-home salary. This protects against job loss risk and lifestyle compression.

## Common Mistakes Salaried Buyers Make

1. **Buying at maximum budget** — leave 20% buffer for emergencies
2. **Ignoring maintenance costs** — Rs 3,000-8,000/month in new societies
3. **Not comparing loan rates** — even 0.25% difference saves Rs 3-5L over 20 years
4. **Buying far from workplace** — commute costs erode savings from cheaper property

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Should salaried employees buy or rent in 2026?

If you plan to stay in a city for 5+ years and can keep EMI under 40% of take-home, buying makes sense. For shorter stays or cities where you're unsure about long-term employment, renting and investing the difference is often better.

### How many years of salary history do banks need for home loans?

Most banks require 2+ years of continuous employment and 3-6 months of salary slips. Job changers should wait 6 months at their new employer before applying. Some banks accept offer letters for IT professionals.

### Should I take a 20-year or 30-year loan?

20-year loans save 30-40% in total interest paid. However, 30-year loans offer lower EMI giving you more breathing room. The optimal strategy: take a 30-year loan but prepay whenever you receive bonuses, reducing the effective tenure.
