## The Rental Yield Problem in Indian Real Estate

Indian property is primarily an appreciation play, not an income play. Average gross rental yields of 2.5-5.0% are low compared to fixed deposits (7-8%) and significantly below global standards (5-8% in developed markets).

## Net Yield After Expenses

Gross yield does not equal actual returns. Deduct these costs:

| Expense | % of Gross Rent |
| --- | --- |
| Property tax | 5-8% |
| Maintenance charges | 8-15% |
| Vacancy (1 month/year avg) | 8% |
| Repairs/upkeep | 5-8% |
| Insurance | 1-2% |
| **Total deductions** | **27-41%** |

**Net yield = Gross yield x (1 - deduction rate)**

| City | Gross Yield | Est. Net Yield | Beat FD? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hyderabad | 4.7% | 2.8-3.4% | No |
| Bangalore | 4.4% | 2.6-3.2% | No |
| Mumbai | 3.2% | 1.9-2.3% | No |

**Key insight:** No Indian city's rental yield beats a fixed deposit on a net basis. The investment thesis for Indian real estate rests on capital appreciation, not rental income.

## When Rental Yield Still Matters

Despite low net yields, rental income serves important functions:

1. **EMI offset** — rental income covering 40-60% of EMI makes leveraged investment viable
2. **Holding cost reduction** — reduces the effective cost of holding an appreciating asset
3. **Vacancy indicator** — high rental demand signals strong capital appreciation potential
