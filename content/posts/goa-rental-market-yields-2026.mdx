## Goa Rental Market: Two Different Games

Goa has two distinct rental markets — vacation/short-term rentals (October to March season) and long-term residential. The economics are fundamentally different.

## Vacation Rental Yields (Short-Term)

| Locality | Avg Nightly Rate | Occupancy (Season) | Annual Gross Yield |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Calangute-Baga (2BHK) | ₹6,000 - ₹10,000 | 75-85% | 6.5-8.0% |
| Anjuna-Vagator (Villa) | ₹12,000 - ₹25,000 | 60-70% | 5.5-7.0% |
| Assagao (Luxury Villa) | ₹15,000 - ₹35,000 | 55-65% | 4.5-6.0% |
| South Goa (2BHK) | ₹4,000 - ₹8,000 | 65-75% | 5.0-6.5% |
| Morjim-Ashwem (Villa) | ₹10,000 - ₹20,000 | 60-70% | 5.5-7.0% |

**Note:** Vacation rental yields are calculated including both high season (October-March) and lean season (April-September). Lean season occupancy drops to 20-35%.

## Vacation Rental vs Long-Term: Which Is Better?

| Factor | Vacation Rental | Long-Term Rental |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Gross yield | 5.5-8.0% | 3.9-4.3% |
| Management effort | High | Low |
| Seasonal variation | Extreme | Stable |
| Maintenance cost | Higher | Normal |
| Regulatory risk | Increasing | Low |
| Furniture required | Yes (fully furnished) | Optional |

**Recommendation:** Vacation rentals offer higher yields but require active management or professional property managers (who charge 15-25% of revenue). For passive income, long-term rentals are simpler.

## Key Insight

Goa offers India's best vacation rental yields (6-8% in prime locations) but requires active management. Long-term yields are comparable to other Tier-2 cities. The sweet spot is North Goa beach proximity apartments with professional management.
