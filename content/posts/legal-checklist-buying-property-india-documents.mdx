## Why Document Verification Is Non-Negotiable

Nearly 30% of property disputes in Indian courts involve title defects that could have been caught with proper due diligence before purchase. Never skip document verification — even with a reputable builder.

## The 15-Document Checklist

### Title Documents

**1. Sale Deed / Title Deed**
The primary ownership document. Verify the complete chain of ownership for at least 30 years. Each transfer should be registered and stamped.

**2. Encumbrance Certificate (EC)**
Obtain from the Sub-Registrar for the past 30 years. EC confirms the property is free from any legal liabilities — mortgages, liens, court attachments.

**3. Mutation Record**
Revenue department record confirming the seller's name as current owner in government records. Also called "khata" (Karnataka), "patta" (Tamil Nadu), or "mutation entry."

### Approval Documents

**4. Approved Building Plan**
From the local planning authority (DTCP, CMDA, HMDA, BDA, etc.). Verify the actual construction matches the approved plan.

**5. RERA Registration Certificate**
Mandatory for all projects above 500 sq mt / 8 units. Verify on state RERA portal.

**6. Commencement Certificate**
Permission to begin construction, issued by the local municipal authority.

**7. Occupation Certificate (OC)**
Issued after construction completion, confirming the building meets approved plan specifications. Mandatory for ready-to-move properties.

### Land Documents

**8. Land Use Certificate / Conversion Order**
Confirms the land is authorized for residential/commercial use. Critical for properties on formerly agricultural land.

**9. NOC from Layout Authority**
If property is in a layout (BDA, HUDA, etc.), verify layout approval and NOC.

**10. Survey Map and Number**
Cross-verify survey number with the actual property location using revenue maps.

### Financial Documents

**11. Property Tax Receipts**
Last 5 years of property tax receipts confirm ownership and rule out municipal claims.

**12. Utility Bill Records**
Electricity, water connection in seller's name confirms occupation.

**13. Outstanding Dues Certificate**
From housing society (if apartment) — confirms no pending maintenance or legal charges.

### Legal Clearances

**14. Court Order Search**
Search district court and high court records for any pending litigation involving the property or seller.

**15. Power of Attorney (if applicable)**
If seller is acting through POA, verify: POA is registered, specific (not general), within validity period, and the principal is alive.

## Red Flags to Walk Away From

- Seller cannot provide original documents (only photocopies)
- Gaps in title chain (unexplained transfers)
- Property under litigation (even "minor" cases)
- Builder not RERA registered
- Pressure to skip legal verification
