## Beyond the Sales Brochure

Every builder looks good in their marketing material. Investment-grade analysis means verifying claims with public data, financial records, and on-ground reality. Here's the framework professional real estate analysts use.

## The 7-Point Builder Assessment Framework

### 1. RERA Delivery Track Record

The single most important metric. Pull data from state RERA portals for every project the builder has registered:

| Rating | On-Time Delivery Rate | Signal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| A+ | 90%+ projects on time | Elite track record |
| A | 75-90% on time | Strong performer |
| B+ | 60-75% on time | Average — proceed with caution |
| B | 40-60% on time | Below average — high risk |
| C | Below 40% on time | Avoid |

### 2. Financial Health Analysis

For listed builders, check quarterly filings. For unlisted, request RERA-filed project accounts:

- **Debt-to-equity ratio** — below 1.5x is healthy, above 2.5x is concerning
- **Interest coverage ratio** — should exceed 2x
- **Cash flow from operations** — must be positive
- **Unsold inventory** — more than 24 months of unsold stock signals trouble

### 3. Land Title Quality

- Is land freehold or leasehold?
- Any litigation on the land parcel?
- Revenue records (7/12, Khata, Patta) verified?
- Encumbrance certificate clear for 30+ years?

### 4. Construction Quality Assessment

Visit completed projects by the same builder. Check:
- External wall and waterproofing quality
- Common area maintenance standards
- Lift and electrical infrastructure
- Plumbing and drainage systems
- Structural quality (any visible cracks, water seepage)

### 5. RERA Complaint History

A builder with more than 50 complaints per 500 units has systemic issues. Categorize complaints:
- Delivery delays (most common)
- Carpet area discrepancies
- Quality defects
- Refund disputes
- False advertising

### 6. Post-Possession Service

How does the builder handle maintenance for the first 5 years?
- Is there a dedicated facility management team?
- Response time for structural defect complaints
- Society formation timeline (should be within 3-6 months of OC)
- Common area completion vs promised amenities

### 7. Resale Market Liquidity

Can you sell the property within 3-6 months if needed?
- Check resale listings for the builder's completed projects
- Talk to brokers about resale demand
- Compare resale prices with launch prices
- Check if banks readily approve loans against the builder's projects

## Red Flags That Professional Analysts Catch

1. Builder launches new projects while existing ones are delayed
2. Frequent entity name changes or SPV structures
3. Marketing claims not matching RERA registration details
4. No quarterly progress updates on RERA portal
5. Builder's own employees won't invest in their projects

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long should builder due diligence take?

A thorough assessment takes 2-3 weeks. This includes RERA verification (1 day), site visits to completed projects (1-2 days), financial analysis (2-3 days), and reference checks with existing buyers (1 week). Don't let a "limited period offer" rush you.

### Are tier-1 builders always safe?

No. Even large, listed developers have delayed projects and faced RERA penalties. Brand size reduces risk but doesn't eliminate it. Apply the same framework regardless of builder reputation.
