## What Drives Rental Demand

### Employment Density
Cities with concentrated IT/manufacturing campuses create clustered rental demand. Hyderabad (Gachibowli), Bangalore (Whitefield/Electronic City), and Pune (Hinjewadi) benefit most.

### Migration Patterns
Net in-migration drives rental demand. Cities attracting 50,000+ migrants annually sustain lower vacancy rates.

### Student Populations
Pune (1M+ students), Bangalore (500K+), and Chennai (400K+) benefit from student rental demand — lower per-unit rent but near-zero vacancy.

## Net Yield Calculation

Gross yield overstates returns. Deduct these costs:

| Cost Component | % of Rent |
| --- | --- |
| Maintenance charges | 8-15% |
| Property tax | 3-5% |
| Vacancy allowance | 5-10% |
| Repairs/upkeep | 3-5% |
| Management fees (if applicable) | 8-12% |

**Net yield = Gross yield - 25-40%** depending on property type and management approach.

## Maximizing Rental Returns

1. **Furnished premium** — furniture investment of Rs 2-4 lakhs can boost rent by 25-35%
2. **Co-living conversion** — converting 3BHK to shared housing can double gross yield
3. **Short-term rentals** — Airbnb/OYO in tourist cities (Goa, Jaipur) can yield 6-8%
4. **IT corridor proximity** — within 5km of major IT park commands 20-30% premium rent

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is rental yield or capital appreciation more important?

Depends on your cash flow needs. Rental yield ensures EMI coverage and passive income. Capital appreciation builds wealth on paper. Ideally, target cities offering 3.5%+ yield with 8%+ appreciation — Hyderabad, Pune, and Bangalore currently hit both marks.

### How do I maximize rental income without increasing investment?

Furnish the property (25-35% rent premium for Rs 2-4L investment), target IT professionals as tenants (stable income, longer stays), and consider co-living format for larger units.

### Are commercial properties better for rental yield?

Commercial properties yield 6-9% gross — significantly higher than residential. However, they require larger capital, have longer vacancy periods, and face GST on rentals above Rs 20L annually.
