## South India's Two IT Hubs: Different Risk Profiles

Bangalore and Chennai are India's #1 and #4 IT employment cities. Both offer IT-driven rental demand, improving metro connectivity, and strong long-term fundamentals. But they differ sharply on price, water security, and growth trajectory.

## Head-to-Head (2026)

| Parameter | Chennai | Bangalore |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Avg Rate (IT corridors) | ₹4,500-9,000/sqft | ₹6,500-13,000/sqft |
| 2BHK Entry Budget | ₹30L-65L | ₹55L-1.1Cr |
| Gross Rental Yield | 3.0-4.0% | 2.5-3.8% |
| 3-Year CAGR | 10-15% | 11-17% |
| IT Employment | 8L+ | 18L+ |
| Water Security | HIGH (desalination) | LOW (Cauvery stress) |
| Traffic | MODERATE | SEVERE |
| Metro | 2 lines + Phase 2 (118 km) | 2 lines + Phase 3 planned |

## The Verdict

**Choose Chennai if:** You want lower entry prices, higher yields, water security, and stability. Budget ₹30-65L. Siruseri and Porur are top picks.

**Choose Bangalore if:** You want higher absolute appreciation, deeper employment market, and more exit liquidity. Budget ₹60L+. Sarjapur Road is the top pick.

**The overlooked factor:** Bangalore's water crisis is a structural risk that the market has not fully priced in. Chennai's desalination infrastructure provides long-term certainty that will become increasingly valuable.

## The Bottom Line

For investors with ₹30-65L budgets seeking stable yields, Chennai is the smarter pick. Bangalore wins on employment scale and appreciation at higher budgets. The water security factor increasingly favours Chennai as a 10+ year hold.
