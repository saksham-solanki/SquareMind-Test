## Tri-City Rental Market: Government Demand Creates Stability

The Chandigarh Tri-City rental market has a unique advantage: government employees on posting transfers create a consistent, non-cyclical tenant base. Add IT professionals from Mohali and NRI-funded PG accommodation demand, and you get a rental market that doesn't swing with economic cycles.

## Top Yield Picks

### Kharar: Highest Yields in the Tri-City

Kharar on the Chandigarh-Ambala highway offers the highest yields because capital values (₹3,500-4,800/sqft) are the lowest in the Tri-City while rental demand from Chandigarh commuters is strong. A 2BHK costing ₹22-32L can rent for ₹8,000-12,000/month.

### Zirakpur: The Balanced Play

Zirakpur's VIP Road corridor offers a good balance of yield (3.2-3.8%) and liquidity. Proximity to NH-22 and the Ambala-Chandigarh Expressway makes it accessible to employers across the Tri-City.

## PG and Student Housing Opportunity

Chandigarh's university ecosystem (PU, NIPER, ISB, IIT Ropar proximity) creates strong PG demand. A 3BHK in Mohali or Zirakpur operated as PG accommodation can generate 2-3x traditional rental income. Co-living operators are entering the market but it remains largely informal.

## The Bottom Line

Tri-City rental yields (3.0-4.0%) are competitive with tier-1 cities at significantly lower capital investment. Kharar and Zirakpur lead for yield, Mohali IT City offers yield + appreciation. Government tenant demand provides a stability floor that IT-only cities lack.
