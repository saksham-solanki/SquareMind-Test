## Why Gross Rental Yield Misleads Investors

Gross rental yield—annual rent divided by property price—dominates casual conversations, but it's a headline number that ignores reality. For a ₹1 crore apartment renting at ₹30,000/month (₹3.6 lakh/year), gross yield is 3.6%. Sounds solid? In practice, expenses erode it dramatically.

In our analysis of over 200 properties, gross yields average 2.5-4% in metros, but **real estate return calculation India** must account for hidden drags. Maintenance at ₹3-12/sqft/month in societies like those in [Electronic City, Bangalore](/insights/bangalore-investment-opportunities-smart-money-2026) can eat 20-30% of rent. Property taxes (0.5-1.5% of assessed value), vacancies (5-10% allowance), repairs (0.5-1% of value), and insurance (₹2,000-5,000/year) slash it further. Result: actual cash returns 40-50% lower.

We recommend ditching gross yield as your sole metric. It's like judging a stock by dividends alone, ignoring growth. For accurate **property investment ROI calculator** insights, layer in net figures and appreciation—especially in high-growth areas like [Sarjapur Road](/insights/bangalore-investment-opportunities-smart-money-2026).

## The Five Essential Metrics for Real Estate ROI

No single number captures **real estate ROI in India**. We use a five-metrics framework at SquareMind, blending income, growth, and leverage effects. This mirrors professional fund strategies and has helped our clients spot winners in [Tier 2 city real estate hidden gems](/insights/tier-2-city-real-estate-hidden-gems-investors).

### 1. Net Rental Yield: Income After Real Costs

**Formula:** (Annual Rent - Annual Expenses) / Total Investment Cost × 100

Start with gross rent, then deduct:
- Maintenance: ₹3-12/sqft/month (higher in gated communities)
- Property tax: 0.5-1.5% annually (varies by municipal corporation)
- Vacancy: 5-10% of rent (longer in oversupplied areas like Andheri)
- Repairs: 0.5-1% of property value
- Insurance: ₹2,000-5,000
- Society charges: ₹2-5/sqft/month

**Example:** ₹1 crore 2BHK in Wagholi, Pune (₹25,000/month rent = ₹3 lakh/year gross, 3%). Expenses: ₹1 lakh maintenance/tax/repairs. Net income: ₹2 lakh. **Net yield: 2%**. In [best cities for rental income India](/insights/best-cities-rental-income-india-2026), this holds at 60-70% of gross.

Net yield assesses pure income viability. In low-yield Mumbai (1.5% net), it's a red flag for cash-flow plays.

### 2. Capital Appreciation (CAGR): The True Wealth Builder

**Formula:** (Current Value / Purchase Price)^(1/years) - 1 × 100

India's uneven markets drive 70-80% of returns here. Based on our tracking:
- Premium metros (Mumbai Goregaon, Gurgaon premium): 4-8% CAGR
- Growth corridors ([Panvel Mumbai](/insights/bullet-train-corridor-property-investment-opportunities), [Hinjewadi Pune](/insights/delhi-ncr-investment-opportunities-smart-money-2026)): 12-18%
- Tier-2 ([Chandigarh Tri-City](/insights/chandigarh-investment-opportunities-smart-money-2026)): 8-14%
- Stagnant (old suburbs): 0-4%

Over 5 years, a ₹50 lakh Sarjapur plot at 16% CAGR grows to ₹108 lakh. We see this in [property appreciation India 2026](/insights/best-cities-capital-appreciation-india-2026) forecasts.

### 3. Total Return: Yield + Appreciation Synergy

**Formula:** Net Yield + CAGR

This gives the blended picture:

|----------|-----------|---------------------|------------------|
| Electronic City, Bangalore | 2.8% | 10% | **12.8%** |
| Sarjapur Road, Bangalore | 2.2% | 16% | **18.2%** |
| Panvel, Mumbai | 2.0% | 15% | **17.0%** |
| Wagholi, Pune | 3.0% | 11% | **14.0%** |
| Andheri, Mumbai | 1.5% | 6% | **7.5%** |

Target 12-15% total for balanced portfolios. Compare with [commercial vs residential real estate investment India](/insights/commercial-vs-residential-real-estate-investment-india) for alternatives.

### 4. Cash-on-Cash Return: Leverage Reality Check

**Formula:** Annual Cash Flow / Total Cash Invested × 100

For financed buys, cash invested = 20-30% (down payment + stamp duty 5-7% + registration 1% + brokerage 1-2%). Cash flow = Rent - EMI - Expenses.

**Example (₹50L property, 80% loan):**

| Leverage | Cash Invested | Annual Cash Flow | Cash-on-Cash |
|----------|---------------|------------------|--------------|
| None | ₹55L | ₹1.2L | **2.2%** |
| 60% LTV | ₹25L | -₹0.5L | **Negative** |
| 80% LTV | ₹15L | -₹1.5L | **Negative** |

Key: Residential leverage kills cash flow; bet on appreciation. Use our [EMI Calculator](/tools/emi-calculator) for scenarios.

### 5. Internal Rate of Return (IRR): The Gold Standard

**Formula:** Discount rate where NPV of cash flows (purchase, rents, expenses, EMIs, sale) = 0.

IRR holistically captures time value. Excel's IRR function or tools work. Typical: 14-20% for 5-year holds in [bullet train corridor property investment](/insights/bullet-train-corridor-property-investment-opportunities).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is real estate ROI in India?

**Real estate ROI** measures profitability as (net profit / total investment) × 100, factoring rental income, appreciation, and costs. In India, averages 8-12% annually per Bajaj Finserv data, but top picks hit 15-20% with proper calculation.

### How to calculate net rental yield?

Subtract expenses (maintenance, tax, vacancy) from gross rent, divide by total cost × 100. For a ₹80L property with ₹2.4L net rent, it's 3%. We see 2-3% typical in metros.

### What is a good ROI for property in India?

Aim for 12-15% total return (yield + appreciation). Below 10% underperforms FDs; above 18% signals growth gems like [Sarjapur Road Bangalore](/insights/bangalore-investment-opportunities-smart-money-2026).

### How does cash-on-cash return differ from total ROI?

Cash-on-cash uses your cash invested (post-leverage), often negative (-5-10%) in residential. Total ROI adds appreciation for the full view.

### Why is IRR the best metric for real estate return calculation India?

IRR accounts for cash flow timing, loans, and exit—most comprehensive. Excel it with inflows/outflows; expect 14-20% in corridors.

### What are common expenses in real estate ROI calculator?

Maintenance (₹3-12/sqft), tax (0.5-1.5%), vacancy (5-10%), repairs (1%), plus transaction costs (8-15%).

### How to factor capital appreciation in ROI?

Use CAGR: (end value/start)^(1/years)-1. Track via [real estate price growth India](/insights/best-cities-capital-appreciation-india-2026) reports.

### Is 3% rental yield good in Mumbai?

Gross yes, but net 1.5-2% + 6% appreciation = 7.5% total—mediocre vs Panvel's 17%.

### How does leverage affect property investment ROI?

Boosts IRR via appreciation but creates negative cash flow. Ideal for 5-7 year holds.

### What is cap rate vs net yield?

Cap rate = NOI / property value (ignores financing); net yield similar but uses total investment.

### How to calculate ROI for flip properties?

Net profit (sale - purchase - costs) / investment. Factor holding costs.

### What risks lower real estate ROI in India?

Vacancy, delays, illiquidity, tax changes. Mitigate via RERA, location.

### When should I use buy vs rent calculator for ROI?

For self-use vs investment; reveals opportunity cost ([Buy vs Rent Calculator](/tools/buy-vs-rent)).

### How do NRIs calculate real estate ROI India?

### How does stamp duty impact ROI?

Adds 5-7% to cost, lowering initial yield 0.2-0.5%. Varies by state; check [Stamp Duty Calculator](/tools/stamp-duty-calculator).

### Is commercial real estate better for ROI than residential?

Often 9-12% yields vs 2-3%, but higher vacancy risk. Compare in [commercial vs residential returns](/insights/commercial-vs-residential-real-estate-investment-india).

### How to project rent growth in IRR?

Assume 5-8% in Tier-1, 3-5% Tier-2, based on inflation + demand.
