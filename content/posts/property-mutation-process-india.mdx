## What Is Mutation?

Property mutation (also called transfer of title in revenue records) is the process of updating government revenue records to reflect the new owner's name after a property sale, inheritance, or gift.

**Important:** Registration of the sale deed transfers ownership legally. Mutation updates the revenue records to reflect this change. Both are necessary.

## Why Mutation Matters

| Without Mutation | With Mutation |
| --- | --- |
| Property tax bills come in old owner's name | Tax in your name |
| Revenue records show old owner | Records show you as owner |
| Future sale may face complications | Clean records for next sale |
| Utility transfers may be difficult | Smoother utility management |
| Potential disputes about ownership | Clear revenue proof |

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Gather Documents
- Registered sale deed (certified copy)
- Previous owner's revenue documents
- Encumbrance Certificate
- Death certificate / succession certificate (if inherited)
- ID proof and address proof

### Step 2: Apply to Revenue Authority
- Visit Tahsildar / Mamlatdar / Revenue office
- Submit mutation application with documents
- Pay application fee (₹100-500 typically)

### Step 3: Verification
- Revenue inspector verifies the property and documents
- Public notice may be issued (15-30 day objection period)
- Spot inspection may be conducted

### Step 4: Order Passed
- If no objections, mutation order is passed
- Revenue records updated with new owner name
- New revenue extract issued

## Red Flags to Watch For

1. **Seller's mutation not done** from their purchase — indicates incomplete chain
2. **Multiple mutations pending** — complex ownership history
3. **Mutation rejected** — may indicate title dispute or document issues
4. **Revenue records show different owner than sale deed** — legal issue

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is mutation mandatory after property purchase?

While not legally "mandatory" in all states, failing to do mutation creates practical problems — property tax, future sale, and utility management all become complicated.

### What is the penalty for not doing mutation?

No direct penalty in most states, but delayed mutation makes the process harder as documents and witnesses may become unavailable. Apply within 6 months of purchase.
