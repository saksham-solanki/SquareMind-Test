## Why Most Mumbai Investors Get Rental Yield Wrong

The typical Mumbai investor buys a ₹2 Crore flat in Andheri, rents it for ₹45,000/month, and believes they have a "good rental property." The math says otherwise: that's a gross yield of 2.7%, and after maintenance, property tax, vacancy, and repairs, the net yield drops to 1.8–2.0%. A fixed deposit does better.

Real rental yield investing in Mumbai requires looking beyond the obvious — finding pockets where capital values are moderate but rental demand is structurally strong due to employment clusters, transit connectivity, and limited supply.

## The Dombivli–Kalyan Belt: Mumbai's Yield Sweet Spot

Dombivli and Kalyan consistently deliver the highest rental yields in MMR for a straightforward reason: capital values remain affordable (₹6,000–8,500/sqft) while rental demand is driven by MIDC industrial workers, logistics staff, and retail sector employees who need affordable housing near their workplaces.

Key advantages for yield investors:

- **Tenant stickiness:** Working-class tenants tend to stay 3–5 years, reducing vacancy and turnover costs
- **Low maintenance buildings:** Affordable housing societies have lower maintenance charges (₹3–5/sqft vs ₹10–15/sqft in premium locations)
- **Multiple income potential:** Some investors buy two 1BHK units instead of one 2BHK, achieving higher aggregate yield

**Builders to consider:** Runwal, Puraniks, Kalpataru (budget segment), Lodha (Palava township in Dombivli).

## Airoli–Ghansoli: The Corporate Rental Play

Airoli and Ghansoli have emerged as surprisingly strong rental markets thanks to Mindspace Airoli, Reliance Corporate Park, and the Navi Mumbai IT cluster. Tenants here are primarily IT professionals earning ₹8–20 LPA who want to live close to work.

- Rental demand is consistent through the year with low seasonal variation
- 2BHK units in the ₹55–70 lakh range can fetch ₹18,000–22,000/month
- Furnished apartments command a 25–30% premium

### Risk: Oversupply in Ghansoli

Ghansoli has seen aggressive new launches that are running ahead of absorption. Be selective — prefer buildings within walking distance of the Ghansoli railway station or with direct Mindspace access.

## Furnished vs Unfurnished: The Premium Equation

In Mumbai, furnishing a 2BHK apartment costs ₹3–5 lakhs. The rental premium for furnished units varies dramatically by location:

| Location Type | Furnishing Cost | Monthly Premium | Payback Period |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Premium (Powai, Andheri) | ₹5–8 lakhs | ₹8,000–12,000 | 4–6 years |
| Mid-segment (Thane, Kharghar) | ₹3–5 lakhs | ₹5,000–8,000 | 3–5 years |
| Affordable (Dombivli, Vasai) | ₹2–3 lakhs | ₹3,000–5,000 | 3–5 years |

The payback period is similar across segments, but the yield boost is proportionally higher in affordable locations because the base rent is lower.

## Maximising Rental Income: Practical Strategies

1. **Buy near railway stations:** In Mumbai, railway connectivity is the #1 tenant priority. Properties within 1 km of a railway station command 15–20% higher rents and have lower vacancy.

2. **Target 1BHK over 2BHK in affordable locations:** Higher yield per sqft, faster tenant acquisition, lower vacancy risk.

3. **Consider co-living formats:** In IT corridors (Airoli, Powai, Andheri), co-living operators will lease your 2BHK at a 40–50% premium over traditional rental, with guaranteed occupancy.
