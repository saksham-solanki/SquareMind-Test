## The 20-Step Home Buying Checklist

Buying your first home is the biggest financial decision most Indians make. This checklist ensures you do not miss any critical step.

## Phase 1: Financial Preparation (Steps 1-5)

### Step 1: Assess Your Financial Health

Calculate your debt-to-income ratio. Banks approve loans when total EMIs (including new home loan) do not exceed 40-50% of net monthly income.

Use our [EMI Calculator](/tools/emi-calculator) to see what loan amount fits your budget.

### Step 2: Check Your CIBIL Score

Minimum 700 for most banks. Above 750 gets you the best interest rates. Check for free on cibil.com.

### Step 3: Calculate Your Total Budget

| Component | Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Property price | Your base budget |
| Stamp duty + registration | 5-11% (varies by state) |
| GST (if under-construction) | 5% of construction value |
| Brokerage | 1-2% |
| Interior + moving | Rs 2-5L |
| Emergency buffer | 3 months EMI |

Use our [Total Cost Calculator](/tools/total-cost) for a complete breakdown.

### Step 4: Arrange Down Payment

Minimum 10-20% of property value. Sources: savings, FD, mutual fund redemption, family contribution (gift deed recommended for tax clarity).

### Step 5: Get Home Loan Pre-Approval

Apply to 2-3 banks simultaneously. Pre-approval letter gives you: exact loan amount, applicable interest rate, and negotiating power with sellers.

## Phase 2: Property Search (Steps 6-10)

### Step 6: Define Location Criteria

Commute time (under 45 min), school proximity (if planning family), hospital access, market/mall nearby, metro/public transport.

### Step 7: Decide Property Type

| Type | Best For | Risk Level |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ready-to-move apartment | Risk-averse buyers | Low |
| Under-construction | Budget-conscious (10-20% cheaper) | Medium |
| Resale | Established localities | Low |
| Plot + construction | Customization lovers | High |

### Step 8: Shortlist 5-8 Properties

Visit at minimum 5 properties before deciding. Never buy the first property you see.

### Step 9: Should You Buy or Rent?

Use our [Buy vs Rent Calculator](/tools/buy-vs-rent) to make a data-driven decision.

### Step 10: Compare Locations

Visit shortlisted properties at different times (morning, evening, weekend) to assess noise, traffic, water supply, and neighborhood feel.

## Phase 3: Verification (Steps 11-15)

### Step 11: Verify RERA Registration
Check on your state's RERA portal. Never buy an unregistered project.

### Step 12: Title Verification
Hire an independent lawyer (not builder-referred). Cost: Rs 10,000-25,000 for complete title search.

### Step 13: Check Builder Track Record
Visit builder's completed projects. Talk to residents. Check delivery timelines vs promises.

### Step 14: Verify Approvals
Building plan approval, commencement certificate, environment clearance, fire NOC.

### Step 15: Physical Inspection
For ready-to-move: check construction quality, water pressure, electrical fittings, common areas, parking.

## Phase 4: Purchase (Steps 16-20)

### Step 16: Negotiate
5-10% negotiation is normal in most markets. Be prepared to walk away.

### Step 17: Book the Property
Pay token/booking amount (1-5% typically). Get receipt with all terms in writing.

### Step 18: Finalize Home Loan
Submit property documents to bank. Get sanction letter. Sign loan agreement.

### Step 19: Execute Sale Agreement
Engage a lawyer to review. Pay stamp duty. Sign agreement. Ensure all terms match verbal commitments.

### Step 20: Register the Property
Visit Sub-Registrar with buyer, seller, and 2 witnesses. Complete biometric verification. Collect registered deed.
