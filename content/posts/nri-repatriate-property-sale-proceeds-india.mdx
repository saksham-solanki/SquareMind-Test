## The Repatriation Process: What Most NRIs Get Wrong

Selling property in India is the easy part. Getting the money out of India legally — with full documentation and RBI compliance — is where NRIs struggle. This guide walks through the exact process.

## How Much Can You Repatriate?

| Source of Purchase Funds | Repatriation Limit | Documentation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| NRE/FCNR account | Original investment amount (unrestricted) | Bank statements showing original remittance |
| NRO account / Indian income | USD 1 million per financial year | CA certificate (Form 15CB) |
| Inherited property | USD 1 million per financial year | Succession documents + CA certificate |
| Maximum residential properties | 2 properties | For amounts exceeding original investment |

## Step-by-Step Repatriation Process

### Step 1: Complete the Sale and Pay Taxes

Ensure buyer deducts appropriate TDS (20% LTCG / 30% STCG). Sale proceeds go to your NRO account.

### Step 2: File Indian Income Tax Return

Declare capital gains, claim exemptions (Section 54, 54EC if applicable), and pay any remaining tax liability.

### Step 3: Obtain CA Certificate (Form 15CB)

A Chartered Accountant verifies: tax has been paid, amount is eligible for repatriation, FEMA compliance is met. Cost: Rs 5,000-15,000.

### Step 4: File Form 15CA Online

File Form 15CA on the Income Tax e-filing portal. This is your declaration to the government about the remittance.

### Step 5: Submit to Bank

Provide your authorized dealer bank (where NRO account is held) with: Form 15CA, Form 15CB, sale deed, TDS certificates, ITR acknowledgment, passport copy.

### Step 6: Bank Processes Remittance

Bank verifies documentation and processes the wire transfer to your overseas account. Timeline: 3-7 working days after document verification.

## Common Repatriation Problems

1. **Missing original remittance proof** — if you cannot prove NRE funds were used, entire amount falls under NRO limit
2. **TDS not deducted correctly** — bank will not process repatriation until tax compliance is verified
3. **USD 1M limit hit** — if selling high-value property, you may need to spread repatriation across financial years
4. **Bank refuses** — some banks are overly cautious; switch to a bank experienced in NRI repatriation (SBI, HDFC)
