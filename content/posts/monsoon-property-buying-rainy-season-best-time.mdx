## The Contrarian Advantage: Buy When Others Won't

Property sales typically drop 20-30% during monsoon months (June-September). Most buyers avoid site visits in rain. This creates the best negotiation window of the year — and reveals construction quality issues that dry weather hides.

## Why Monsoon Site Visits Are Superior

### What Rain Reveals

| Issue | Visible in Dry Weather? | Visible in Monsoon? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Water seepage through walls | Rarely | Clearly visible |
| Roof/terrace leakage | No | Yes |
| Drainage system quality | No | Obvious |
| Waterlogging in parking/basement | No | Critical check |
| Road access during heavy rain | No | Yes |
| Surrounding area flooding | No | Visible |
| Window/door seal quality | No | Test naturally |

A 30-minute site visit during heavy rain tells you more about construction quality than 3 hours of inspection in sunshine.

## Negotiation Power in Monsoon

### Builder Side
- Quarterly sales targets still apply — fewer walk-ins means more pressure per buyer
- Cash flow concerns increase with slower sales
- Year-end (September) targets create urgency
- Marketing spend wasted on fewer leads

### Buyer Leverage
- Typical monsoon discount: 3-7% off asking price
- Additional freebies: parking, modular kitchen, stamp duty reimbursement
- Better unit selection — premium floors/views available as other buyers wait
- Faster paperwork — registrar offices are less crowded

## Monsoon Due Diligence Checklist

1. **Visit during or immediately after heavy rain** — not just drizzle
2. **Check basement and parking** — any standing water indicates drainage failure
3. **Inspect external walls of completed towers** — water stains reveal seepage
4. **Walk the approach road** — is it accessible during waterlogging?
5. **Check stormwater drains** — are they built and functional?
6. **Talk to existing residents** (in phased projects) — ask about last year's monsoon issues
7. **Review the elevation** — is the project at a natural water collection point?

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Won't construction quality be worse during monsoon?

You're buying, not building. Monsoon visits help evaluate already-constructed quality. For under-construction projects, work slows during rain but that doesn't affect structural quality — concrete actually cures better in humid conditions.

### Should I avoid coastal properties entirely during monsoon visits?

No. Coastal visits during monsoon are the best time to assess flood risk, sea erosion proximity, and drainage effectiveness. If the property handles monsoon well, it's a strong signal.

### Is monsoon registration legally risky?

No. Property registration is a legal process unaffected by weather. All government offices function normally during monsoon. The only practical difference is commute inconvenience to registrar offices.
