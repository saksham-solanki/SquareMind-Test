## How Much Can You Actually Negotiate?

| Market Condition | New Projects | Resale |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Buyer's market (slow sales) | 8-15% | 10-20% |
| Neutral market | 5-8% | 5-10% |
| Seller's market (high demand) | 2-5% | 3-7% |

The realistic negotiation range in most Indian markets in 2026 is 5-10%. Expecting more than 15% discount is usually unrealistic unless the seller is distressed.

## Preparation Before Negotiation

### Know the Market Rate

1. Check circle rate / Ready Reckoner value for the area
2. Compare similar properties on real estate portals (Magicbricks, 99acres)
3. Talk to brokers in the area about recent transaction prices
4. Use our [Stamp Duty Calculator](/tools/stamp-duty-calculator) to understand the government's valuation

### Know Your Budget

Use our [EMI Calculator](/tools/emi-calculator) to know your exact maximum. Never reveal your maximum budget during negotiation.

## Builder Negotiation Tactics

### 1. Ask for the Right Things

Instead of asking for lower price (which builders resist for rate card integrity), ask for:
- **Free car parking** (saves Rs 3-5L)
- **Stamp duty absorption** (builder pays stamp duty)
- **Free club membership** (Rs 1-3L value)
- **Upgraded flooring or modular kitchen**
- **Maintenance-free period** (12-24 months)

### 2. Timing Matters

- **Quarter end (March, June, Sep, Dec):** Builders have targets — more flexible
- **Slow season (July-August):** Monsoon = fewer site visits = more deals
- **Year end (March):** Financial year closing, maximum flexibility

### 3. Leverage Points

- Cash/ready buyer (no loan delay)
- Multiple unit purchase
- Referral from existing buyer
- Competitor quote from nearby project

## Resale Negotiation Tactics

1. **Point out negatives:** Minor issues (paint, fixtures) give negotiating ammunition
2. **Ask about urgency:** Sellers relocating or needing funds urgently negotiate more
3. **Make conditional offers:** "I will close in 30 days if you accept Rs X"
4. **Walk away (and mean it):** The most powerful negotiation tool

## Walk-Away Signals

Do not pursue a deal if:
- Seller refuses to provide documents for verification
- Price is 20%+ above comparable recent transactions
- Builder has poor RERA delivery record
- Property has encumbrance or legal issues
