## TDS on NRI Rental Income

When an NRI rents out property in India, the tenant must deduct TDS at 30% (plus applicable cess) before paying rent. This applies whether the tenant is an individual, company, or any other entity.

**Key difference from residents:** For resident landlords, TDS on rent is only deducted when monthly rent exceeds Rs 50,000. For NRI landlords, TDS applies on all rental payments regardless of amount.

## Deductions NRIs Can Claim

| Deduction | Amount | Section |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Municipal taxes | Actual amount paid | Standard |
| Standard deduction | 30% of net annual value | Section 24(a) |
| Home loan interest | Unlimited for let-out property | Section 24(b) |

After these deductions, effective tax rate on rental income is significantly lower than the 30% TDS rate — which is why filing ITR for refund is important.

## How to File ITR for Rental Income

1. Obtain Form 26AS from the Income Tax portal (shows TDS deducted by tenant)
2. Calculate taxable rental income after deductions
3. File ITR-2 (applicable for NRIs with property income)
4. Claim refund of excess TDS
5. E-verify using Aadhaar OTP or DSC

## DTAA Relief for Double Taxation

If your country of residence also taxes worldwide income, you can claim Indian taxes paid as a credit against your home country tax liability under DTAA.

| Country | DTAA Treatment |
| --- | --- |
| US | Report on Schedule E (1040), claim Foreign Tax Credit |
| UK | Report on Self Assessment, claim credit for Indian tax |
| Canada | Report on T776, claim Foreign Tax Credit |
| UAE | No income tax — Indian tax is the only liability |
| Australia | Report on tax return, claim Foreign Income Tax Offset |

## Tenant's TDS Obligations

Your tenant must: obtain TAN (Tax Deduction Account Number), deduct 30% TDS, deposit with government quarterly using Form 27Q, issue Form 16A to you annually.
