## Bangalore Stamp Duty Rates (2026)

Karnataka has a straightforward stamp duty structure with no gender-based concession:

| Component | Rate |
| --- | --- |
| Stamp duty | 5% |
| Registration charge | 1% |
| Surcharge (BBMP limits, above Rs 45L) | 2% |
| Cess | 0.1% |
| **Total (below Rs 45L)** | **6.1%** |
| **Total (above Rs 45L in BBMP)** | **8.1%** |

The 2% surcharge on properties above Rs 45 lakhs within BBMP limits adds significantly to acquisition cost in Bangalore's premium areas.

## Guidance Value System

Karnataka uses "Guidance Value" (equivalent to circle rate) published by the Department of Stamps and Registration. Stamp duty is calculated on the higher of: actual transaction value or guidance value.

Check guidance values at: kaverionline.karnataka.gov.in

## Online Registration via Kaveri Portal

1. Visit kaveri.karnataka.gov.in
2. Create account with Aadhaar
3. Enter property details (SRO, village, survey number)
4. System auto-calculates stamp duty based on guidance value
5. Pay stamp duty and registration online
6. Book SRO appointment
7. Complete biometric registration at Sub-Registrar Office

Karnataka's Kaveri 2.0 portal is considered one of the most efficient in India for property registration.

## Common Issues in Karnataka

1. Guidance value outdated — actual market rate much higher in fast-growing areas
2. Khata transfer fees (A Khata vs B Khata) — additional municipal cost
3. Betterment charges in BDA layouts
4. BBMP property tax arrears from previous owner
