## When Environmental Clearance Is Required

Environmental clearance (EC) from the State Environment Impact Assessment Authority (SEIAA) or Ministry of Environment (MoEF) is mandatory for large-scale construction projects.

## Project Size Thresholds

| Project Type | Threshold for EC | Authority |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Building & construction | > 20,000 sqm built-up area | SEIAA (state level) |
| Township projects | > 50 hectares or > 1,50,000 sqm built-up | MoEF (central level) |
| Industrial projects | All sizes | SEIAA/MoEF depending on category |

**Key point:** If you're buying in a project above 20,000 sqm built-up area (most mid-large apartment complexes), the builder must have environmental clearance.

## Coastal Regulation Zone (CRZ) Rules

CRZ rules apply to all properties within 500 meters of the High Tide Line (HTL) on the coast.

### CRZ Classifications

| Zone | What It Means | Construction Allowed? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CRZ-I | Ecologically sensitive (mangroves, coral reefs) | No |
| CRZ-II | Developed urban area with existing infrastructure | Yes, per existing norms |
| CRZ-III | Rural area within 500m of HTL | No construction within 200m of HTL |
| CRZ-IV | Water area (sea, tidal influenced) | No |

### Cities Most Affected by CRZ

| City | CRZ Impact Areas |
| --- | --- |
| Mumbai | Entire western coastline, Nariman Point to Versova |
| Chennai | Marina Beach to ECR corridor |
| Goa | All beach-facing areas, both coasts |
| Kochi | Backwater-adjacent properties |
| Vizag | Beach road corridor |

## How to Verify CRZ Compliance

1. **Check the approved CRZ map** for the area (available with Coastal Zone Management Authority)
2. **Request CRZ NOC** from the developer (if property is coastal)
3. **Verify with local planning authority** that building permission accounts for CRZ restrictions
4. **Physical inspection** — if you can see the sea from the property, CRZ verification is mandatory

## How to Verify Environmental Clearance

1. **PARIVESH Portal:** parivesh.nic.in — search by project name or company
2. **Request from builder:** Ask for EC letter and conditions
3. **Check compliance reports:** EC comes with conditions — verify the builder is meeting them

## Red Flags to Watch For

1. **No EC for large project** — project may face stop-work orders
2. **CRZ violation** — demolition orders from CZMA are enforceable
3. **EC conditions not met** — water treatment plant not built, green cover not maintained
4. **Construction within CRZ-I** — illegal, no exceptions
5. **Builder claims "EC not required"** for a large project — verify threshold yourself

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can CRZ regulations change after I buy?

CRZ regulations are updated periodically (last major update: CRZ 2019 notification). Existing constructions with valid approvals are generally protected. However, new CRZ rules can restrict modifications or redevelopment.

### What happens if my building violates CRZ rules?

CZMA can issue demolition orders. Several high-profile demolitions have occurred (Maradu apartments in Kochi, 2020). CRZ violations are taken seriously and court orders for demolition are enforceable.
