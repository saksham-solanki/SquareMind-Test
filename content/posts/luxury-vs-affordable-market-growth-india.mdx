## Two Markets, Two Stories

India's residential real estate has bifurcated into two distinct markets with different growth trajectories, buyer profiles, and investment characteristics.

## Segment Definition

| Segment | Price Range | Typical Unit Size | Target Buyer |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Affordable | ₹25-50 L | 500-800 sqft | First-time buyers, migrant workers |
| Mid-segment | ₹50 L - ₹1.5 Cr | 800-1,400 sqft | IT professionals, dual-income families |
| Premium | ₹1.5-4 Cr | 1,400-2,500 sqft | Senior professionals, HNIs |
| Luxury | ₹4 Cr+ | 2,500+ sqft | Ultra-HNIs, NRIs, corporate executives |

## Growth Comparison (2022-2025)

| Metric | Affordable | Mid | Premium | Luxury |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Volume growth (CAGR) | +5.2% | +12.8% | +18.5% | +22.3% |
| Price appreciation (CAGR) | +6.5% | +9.8% | +12.2% | +14.5% |
| New launches share | 22% | 38% | 28% | 12% |
| Absorption rate | 68% | 78% | 82% | 75% |

**Key finding:** Luxury and premium segments are growing fastest in both volume and price. Affordable housing growth has slowed despite government incentives.

## Why Luxury Is Winning

1. **Post-COVID upgrade demand** — buyers prioritising space and amenities
2. **Wealth concentration** — top 10% income growth outpacing average
3. **NRI demand** — weak rupee makes Indian luxury attractive
4. **Developer preference** — higher margins incentivise luxury launches

## Why Affordable Is Struggling

1. **Input cost inflation** — construction costs up 25-30%, squeezing affordable margins
2. **Land cost escalation** — land prices have risen faster than affordable ceiling
3. **PMAY tapering** — government subsidy scheme ending
4. **Developer exit** — many developers abandoning affordable for premium

## Investment Implications

| Factor | Affordable | Luxury |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Entry cost | Low (₹25-50L) | Very high (₹4Cr+) |
| Appreciation | Moderate (6-8%) | High (12-15%) |
| Rental yield | Higher (4-5%) | Lower (2-3%) |
| Liquidity | Lower | Higher |
| Tenant quality | Variable | Premium |
| Maintenance cost | Low | Very high |

**For investors:** Mid-segment (₹50L-1.5Cr) offers the best risk-adjusted returns — strong demand, reasonable entry cost, and good appreciation.
