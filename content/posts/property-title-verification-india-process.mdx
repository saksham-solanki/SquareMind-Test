## Why Title Verification Is Non-Negotiable

Title defects are discovered in approximately 1 in 8 property transactions in India. A defective title means you could lose both the property and your money, regardless of how much you paid.

## What Is a "Clear Title"?

A clear title means:
1. The seller has legal right to sell the property
2. No other person or entity has a competing claim
3. No encumbrances (mortgages, liens, or charges) exist
4. The property is free from litigation
5. All previous transfers were legally valid

## Step-by-Step Title Verification

### Step 1: Obtain the Complete Document Chain

Request from the seller:
- Original sale deed (current and previous)
- All previous conveyance/transfer documents going back 30+ years
- Partition deed (if property was divided among family members)
- Will / succession certificate (if inherited)
- Power of Attorney (if applicable)
- Development agreement (if builder project)

### Step 2: Verify at the Sub-Registrar Office

- Visit the sub-registrar office of the property's jurisdiction
- Request certified copies of all registered documents
- Verify the seal, registration number, and dates match
- Check the Encumbrance Certificate (EC) for 30 years

### Step 3: Cross-Check Revenue Records

| State | Key Revenue Document | Online Portal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Maharashtra | 7/12 extract + Property card | mahabhulekh.maharashtra.gov.in |
| Karnataka | RTC (Record of Rights) | landrecords.karnataka.gov.in |
| Tamil Nadu | Patta + Chitta | tnlandsurvey.tn.gov.in |
| Telangana | Dharani records | dharani.telangana.gov.in |
| Gujarat | 7/12 + Village form 6 | anyror.gujarat.gov.in |
| UP/Haryana | Khasra/Khatauni | Revenue department portals |

### Step 4: Legal Opinion from Property Lawyer

After gathering documents, get a formal title opinion from a property lawyer who:
- Specialises in the state where property is located
- Has reviewed the complete chain of documents
- Provides written opinion on title marketability
- Identifies any defects or risks

**Cost:** ₹5,000-25,000 depending on property value and complexity. Worth every rupee.

## Common Title Defects

| Defect | Description | Severity |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Missing link in chain | Gap in ownership transfer history | Critical |
| Forged documents | Fake sale deeds or powers of attorney | Critical |
| Undivided property | Co-owners haven't formally partitioned | High |
| Pending litigation | Court case involving the property | High |
| Unauthorised construction | Built without proper approvals | Moderate |
| Encumbrance not cleared | Old mortgage not formally released | Moderate |

## Red Flags to Watch For

1. **Seller rushing the deal** — legitimate sellers don't pressure for immediate payment
2. **Only photocopies available** — always demand original documents
3. **Frequent transfers in short period** — may indicate fraud or speculation
4. **Power of Attorney based sale** — higher fraud risk; verify POA registration
5. **No EC or gaps in EC** — indicates unregistered transactions in between

## Title Insurance

Title insurance is available in India (offered by companies like Stewart India, SBI General) and covers:
- Legal defense costs if title is challenged
- Financial loss if title is proven defective
- Cost of clearing title defects

**Premium:** 0.5-1% of property value, one-time payment. Recommended for properties above ₹1 Cr.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How far back should title search go?

Minimum 30 years. For properties in litigation-prone areas or with complex ownership history, 40-50 years is recommended.

### Can I do title verification myself?

You can do preliminary checks (EC, revenue records, RERA) yourself. However, a property lawyer should review the complete chain and provide a formal title opinion. Self-verification is not sufficient for high-value purchases.
