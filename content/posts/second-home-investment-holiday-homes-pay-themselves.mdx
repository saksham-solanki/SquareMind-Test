## The Second Home Dream vs The Second Home Math

Every urban Indian dreams of a holiday home — a villa in Goa, a cottage in Lonavala, a farmhouse in Alibaug. The question: can it pay for itself through rental income while you're not using it?

The short answer: only in specific locations with strong short-term rental demand and reasonable pricing.

## When It Works: Goa North

North Goa (Anjuna, Vagator, Assagao) has the strongest short-term rental market in India. Year-round tourist flow (domestic + international), established Airbnb/Booking.com demand, and professional property management services make it possible to achieve 5-8% gross yield — significantly above urban residential.

**The math for a ₹1Cr 2BHK villa in North Goa:**
- 180 occupied nights at avg ₹6,000/night = ₹10.8L annual income
- Management fees (20-25%): ₹2.2L
- Maintenance + utilities: ₹1.5L
- Net income: ₹7.1L = 7.1% gross yield

You use it 30-40 days/year, and it pays for itself the rest of the time.

## When It Doesn't Work: Most Places

Most second home locations fail the investment test because:
- **Occupancy below 40%:** Beautiful but not popular enough for consistent bookings
- **Seasonal demand:** 3-4 months of high season, dead the rest
- **Management hassles:** No professional management, you're stuck handling everything remotely
- **Capital appreciation:** Hill stations and beach towns appreciate 3-5% annually — below inflation-adjusted returns

## The Honest Recommendation

Buy a second home if:
1. You'll use it 30+ days/year (personal enjoyment has value)
2. The location has proven short-term rental demand (Airbnb occupancy data available)
3. Professional property management exists locally
4. You can afford the EMI even with zero rental income

**Don't buy a second home as a pure investment.** Urban growth corridor properties almost always deliver better risk-adjusted returns. A ₹1Cr apartment in Sarjapur gives 3.2% yield + 16% CAGR. A ₹1Cr villa in most hill stations gives 3% yield + 4% CAGR.

## The Bottom Line

Second homes that pay for themselves exist — primarily in Goa, select hill stations, and Coorg. But they require active management, seasonal tolerance, and location-specific knowledge. For pure investment returns, stick to urban growth corridors and treat the second home as a lifestyle purchase with partial income offset.
