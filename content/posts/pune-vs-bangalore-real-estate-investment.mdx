## Two IT Powerhouses, Two Very Different Price Points

Pune and Bangalore are India's second and first-largest IT employers respectively. Both cities attract similar tenant profiles — young IT professionals earning ₹8–25 LPA who need quality housing near work. The critical difference: Pune IT corridor properties cost 25–40% less than equivalent Bangalore locations.

| Parameter | Pune | Bangalore |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Avg Rate (IT corridors) | ₹5,500–9,000/sqft | ₹6,500–13,000/sqft |

## The Verdict

**Choose Pune if:** Budget is ₹35–70L, you want higher yields, and you value affordability. Hinjewadi Phase 3 and Kharadi are top picks.

**Choose Bangalore if:** Budget is ₹80L+, you want deeper market liquidity, and you can accept lower yields for potentially higher appreciation. Sarjapur Road is the top pick.

## The Bottom Line

For most investors with ₹40–80L budgets, Pune offers better value per rupee invested. Bangalore wins on liquidity and employment depth at higher budgets. The smartest strategy: pick the city where your budget gives you a quality 2BHK in an IT corridor from a reputed builder — that's the formula that works in both cities.
