## Why NRIs Are Investing in Chennai in 2026

Chennai offers stable property appreciation, strong manufacturing and IT sector tenant demand, and relatively predictable regulatory processes. The city's auto corridor (Ford, Hyundai, BMW) and IT corridor (OMR/SH 49A) provide diversified rental demand beyond just tech.

### Emerging Investment Zones

Sholinganallur, Perungalathur, and Guduvanchery offer 30-50% lower prices. Chennai Metro Phase 2 and Southern Peripheral Road development drive appreciation potential.

## Tamil Nadu Stamp Duty and Registration

Tamil Nadu charges 7% stamp duty plus 4% registration charge — among the highest in India. Women buyers pay 1% less stamp duty (6%). Total acquisition cost including stamp duty and registration can reach 11% of property value.

## Top Builders in Chennai

Casagrand, Alliance Group, Radiance Realty, and Appaswamy Real Estates. Verify:

1. TN-RERA registration on tnrera.in
2. Builder track record in Chennai
3. NRI documentation support
4. NRI home loan bank partnerships

## FEMA Compliance

Standard [FEMA rules](/insights/nri-buying-property-india-guide-2026) apply. Chennai-specific:

- Patta and Chitta documents must be verified for land purchases
- Unapproved layouts are common in Chennai suburbs — verify CMDA/DTCP approval
- POA must be Consulate-attested and registered at Tamil Nadu Sub-Registrar

## NRI Tax Implications

- **Rental income:** 30% TDS. Chennai rental yields of 3-4% on OMR corridor
- **Capital gains:** 12.5% LTCG. 20% TDS on sale
- **Property tax:** Chennai Corporation tax payable biannually

## Property Verification

1. **Title search** from independent Chennai lawyer
2. **Encumbrance certificate** from Tamil Nadu Sub-Registrar (30 years)
3. **TN-RERA registration** verification
4. **Patta transfer** and chitta verification
5. **CMDA/DTCP approval** for layout and building plan
