## Pune's Rental Yield Advantage Over Bangalore and Mumbai

Pune delivers consistently higher rental yields than its larger counterparts for a simple reason: capital values are 30–50% lower while rental demand from IT professionals is proportionally similar. A 2BHK in Hinjewadi renting for ₹16,000–20,000/month costs ₹45–65L — yielding 3.2–4.0%. The same yield in Bangalore requires buying in Electronic City or Sarjapur outskirts.

## Top Yield Plays

### Wagholi — Best Numbers in Pune

Wagholi benefits from Kharadi IT spillover and direct employment at EON IT Park. At ₹35–48L for a 2BHK, rents of ₹9,000–13,000 deliver Pune's highest yields. Tenants are typically IT professionals in the ₹5–12 LPA range who prioritise proximity to work over lifestyle amenities.

**Risk:** Infrastructure gaps — roads and water supply are still catching up. Buy in gated communities from Kolte-Patil, VTP, or Gera that have independent infrastructure.

### Hinjewadi Phase 2-3 — The Walk-to-Work Premium

IT professionals willingly pay 15–20% rent premium for walking-distance proximity to Hinjewadi IT Park. Phase 2-3 projects by Godrej (24), VTP (Blue Waters), and Kolte-Patil (Life Republic) attract sticky tenants with 2–3 year average stays.

## PG and Co-Living Opportunity

Pune's large student and early-career population creates strong demand for PG (paying guest) accommodation. A 3BHK in Hinjewadi costing ₹60–75L can generate ₹35,000–45,000/month when operated as PG (3–4 beds), significantly above the ₹16,000–20,000 traditional rental.

**Caveat:** PG operation requires active management. Co-living operators (Colive, Stanza) offer a middle ground with guaranteed rental and zero management.

Pune's rental yields — consistently 3.0–4.4% gross across IT corridors — make it one of India's best cities for rental income investing. Wagholi and Hinjewadi Phase 2-3 offer the highest yields, while Kharadi provides a balanced yield-appreciation profile. For sub-₹50L budgets seeking rental income, Pune is hard to beat.
