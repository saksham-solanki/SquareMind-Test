## The 8 Most Common Property Scams in India

### 1. Area Inflation

**The scam:** Builder advertises 1200 sq ft "super built-up area." Actual carpet area is only 750 sq ft. You pay for 60% more space than you actually get.

**Protection:** RERA mandates sale on carpet area only. Verify carpet area matches RERA-registered specification. Use measuring tape during site visit.

### 2. Title Fraud

**The scam:** Property sold with forged documents. The actual owner appears later and claims rightful ownership. Buyer loses money and property.

**Protection:** Hire an independent lawyer for title search (30 years). Verify original documents (not photocopies). Check encumbrance certificate.

### 3. Multiple Selling

**The scam:** Same property sold to multiple buyers. First to register wins; others lose their money.

**Protection:** Register immediately after agreement. Do not delay registration based on builder's request. Check encumbrance certificate on registration date.

### 4. GPA (General Power of Attorney) Sales

**The scam:** Property sold through GPA without proper registration. Supreme Court has held that GPA sales are not valid for property transfer.

**Protection:** Never buy property through GPA. Insist on registered sale deed with stamp duty paid.

### 5. Fake RERA Registration

**The scam:** Builder shows a RERA number that is either fake, belongs to another project, or is expired.

**Protection:** Verify the exact RERA number on the state RERA portal yourself. Do not trust builder-provided screenshots.

### 6. Unapproved Construction

**The scam:** Builder constructs additional floors or units beyond approved plan. Municipal corporation can demolish unauthorized construction.

**Protection:** Verify approved building plan from local authority. Count actual floors vs approved floors.

### 7. Delayed Possession Tactics

**The scam:** Builder delays possession by 2-5 years while collecting payments. Penalty clauses are weak or absent in agreement.

**Protection:** Check builder's RERA delivery record. Insist on strong penalty clause in agreement. Buy from builders with proven track records.

### 8. Society Formation Delay

**The scam:** Builder delays society formation, retaining control of common areas and charging inflated maintenance.

**Protection:** RERA mandates society formation within 3 months of majority unit occupation. File RERA complaint if delayed.

## 8 Protection Steps

1. Verify RERA registration independently
2. Hire independent lawyer for title search
3. Never pay in cash
4. Get everything in writing (verbal promises mean nothing)
5. Check builder track record (visit completed projects)
6. Read agreement carefully (every clause)
7. Register property immediately
8. Do not buy through GPA
