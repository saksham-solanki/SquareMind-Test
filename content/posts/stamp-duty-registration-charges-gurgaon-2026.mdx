## Gurgaon Stamp Duty Rates (2026)

| Category | Rate |
| --- | --- |
| Male buyer | 7% |
| Female buyer (sole) | 5% |
| Joint (male + female) | 6% |
| Registration charge | Rs 50,000 (flat for urban) |

Haryana offers a generous 2% concession for women buyers — one of the highest gender-based concessions in India.

## Collector Rate (Circle Rate)

Haryana uses "Collector Rates" as minimum valuation. Updated periodically by the DC (Deputy Commissioner) office. Stamp duty calculated on the higher of: transaction value or collector rate.

Key Gurgaon collector rates (2026):

| Area/Sector | Collector Rate (per sq yd) |
| --- | --- |
| Sectors 42-57 (Golf Course, Sohna Rd) | Rs 85,000-1,20,000 |
| Dwarka Expressway sectors | Rs 50,000-75,000 |
| New Gurgaon (Sector 79-95) | Rs 35,000-55,000 |
| SPR (Southern Peripheral Road) | Rs 30,000-50,000 |

## Registration Process

1. Visit jamabandi.nic.in (Haryana e-Registration)
2. Create account
3. Enter property details and buyer/seller information
4. Pay stamp duty online
5. Book Tehsildar office appointment
6. Complete biometric registration

## Gurgaon-Specific Issues

1. **Colony license verification:** Many Gurgaon projects are on licensed colonies — verify license validity
2. **CLU (Change of Land Use):** Essential for commercial properties on residential land
3. **EDC/IDC charges:** External/Internal Development Charges may be pending on some projects
4. **Flat rate registration:** Rs 50,000 flat (unlike percentage-based in other states) benefits high-value transactions
