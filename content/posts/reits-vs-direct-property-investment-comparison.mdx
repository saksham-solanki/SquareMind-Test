## REITs Changed the Game — But Didn't Replace Direct Investment

India's REIT market (Embassy REIT, Mindspace REIT, Brookfield REIT) has given investors liquid access to commercial real estate. But REITs and direct property investment serve different purposes in a portfolio. Understanding when to use each is worth more than picking between them.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

| Factor | REITs | Direct Property |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Minimum investment | ₹15,000 (1 unit) | ₹25L-50L+ |
| Annual yield | 6-7% (distribution) | 2-4% (residential), 6-9% (commercial) |
| Capital appreciation | 3-5% NAV growth | 8-16% (residential), 5-10% (commercial) |
| Total return | 9-12% | 10-20% (leveraged) |
| Liquidity | HIGH (sell on exchange) | LOW (2-6 months to sell) |
| Management effort | Zero | MEDIUM-HIGH |
| Leverage | Not available | 60-80% LTV loans |
| Tax on distributions | Taxed as income | Rental income taxed as income |
| Tax on gains | 12.5% LTCG (12+ months) | 20% LTCG with indexation (24+ months) |

## When REITs Win

- **Small budgets (under ₹25L):** REITs provide real estate exposure impossible through direct investment
- **Passive investors:** Zero management, zero tenant hassles, zero maintenance
- **Liquidity needs:** Can sell in minutes, not months
- **Diversification:** Single REIT unit gives exposure to 20-40 commercial properties across cities

## When Direct Investment Wins

- **Leverage benefit:** ₹15L cash (₹75L property with 80% loan) generates returns on ₹75L — REITs don't offer leverage
- **Higher total returns:** Leveraged residential in growth corridors delivers 15-20% ROE vs 9-12% from REITs
- **Control:** You choose the city, location, builder, tenant
- **Tax efficiency:** Indexed LTCG on direct property sale can be lower effective tax than REIT distributions taxed as income

## Optimal Portfolio Allocation

| Total RE Budget | Direct Property | REITs | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Under ₹25L | 0% | 100% | Below direct investment minimum |
| ₹25L-1Cr | 80% | 20% | Direct for leverage, REIT for diversification |
| ₹1-3Cr | 70% | 30% | Balanced approach |
| ₹3Cr+ | 60% | 40% | REITs for income, direct for appreciation |

## The Bottom Line

REITs and direct property are complementary, not competitive. Use REITs for liquidity, passive income, and small-ticket diversification. Use direct investment for leverage, appreciation, and control. The optimal portfolio includes both.
