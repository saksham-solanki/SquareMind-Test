## Crowdfunding Real Estate: Promise vs Reality in India

Real estate crowdfunding platforms promise access to property investment from ₹5-25 lakhs, professional management, and returns of 12-18%. The reality is more nuanced — regulatory uncertainty, platform risk, and illiquidity require careful evaluation.

## How It Works in India

1. Platform curates a property (typically commercial or residential development)
2. Investors pool ₹5-25L each to fund the acquisition
3. Platform manages the property (tenant acquisition, maintenance)
4. Investors receive proportional rental income + capital appreciation at exit
5. Typical hold period: 3-7 years

## The Risk Reality

1. **Regulatory framework:** SEBI's SM REIT guidelines (2024) provide some structure, but most platforms operate in a gray area
2. **Illiquidity:** No guaranteed secondary market — your money may be locked for 3-7 years
3. **Platform risk:** If the platform fails, property management continuity is uncertain
4. **Valuation risk:** Exit valuations are platform-determined, not market-transparent
5. **Tax complexity:** Rental distributions taxed as income, capital gains on exit vary by structure

## Our Assessment

Crowdfunding is a **supplementary** investment channel, not a primary one. Allocate 10-15% of your real estate budget maximum. Stick to platforms with 3+ year track records and verified exits.

**Better alternatives at similar ticket sizes:**
- ₹10-25L: REITs (Embassy, Mindspace) — regulated, liquid, similar returns
- ₹25-50L: Direct residential in tier-2 IT corridors — full ownership, RERA protection

## The Bottom Line

Real estate crowdfunding adds an option for small-ticket commercial exposure, but regulatory immaturity and illiquidity limit its role. Use REITs for liquid commercial exposure and direct investment for full control. Crowdfunding occupies a niche for investors who want specific property selection without full capital outlay.
