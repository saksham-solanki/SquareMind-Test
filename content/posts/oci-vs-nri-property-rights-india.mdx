## OCI and NRI: Same Property Rights, Different Identity

Under FEMA, OCI (Overseas Citizen of India) cardholders have the same property purchase rights as NRIs. Both can buy residential and commercial property without RBI approval. Both are restricted from purchasing agricultural land, farmhouses, and plantation property.

## Key Differences

| Feature | NRI | OCI |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Definition | Indian citizen living abroad 182+ days | Foreign citizen with Indian origin |
| Indian passport | Yes | No (foreign passport + OCI card) |
| Property rights | Residential + Commercial | Residential + Commercial |
| Agricultural land | RBI approval needed | RBI approval needed |
| Voting rights | Yes (in person) | No |
| Tax treatment | Same | Same |
| Bank accounts | NRE/NRO/FCNR | NRE/NRO/FCNR |

## Common Misconceptions

### "OCI holders cannot buy property in India"

False. OCI holders have the same property rights as NRIs under FEMA. The only restriction is agricultural land, farmhouses, and plantation property — same as NRIs.

### "OCI holders pay higher stamp duty"

False. Stamp duty is determined by state law and property value, not buyer's citizenship status. OCI holders pay the same stamp duty as residents and NRIs.

### "OCI holders cannot get home loans in India"

Mostly true in practice. While not legally prohibited, most Indian banks are reluctant to offer home loans to OCI holders without Indian income proof. Some banks (SBI, HDFC) have specific OCI loan products but with stricter criteria.

## Tax Treatment

OCI holders are taxed identically to NRIs on Indian property income:

- Rental income: 30% TDS by tenant
- LTCG: 12.5% without indexation
- STCG: Slab rate
- Repatriation: Same FEMA rules as NRIs

## When OCI Status Matters

1. **Inheritance:** OCI holders can inherit agricultural land (cannot purchase it)
2. **Duration of stay:** OCI holders visiting India for property transactions do not need visa
3. **Multiple entries:** OCI card provides lifetime multiple-entry visa
