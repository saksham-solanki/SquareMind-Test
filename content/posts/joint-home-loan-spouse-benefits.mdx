## Why Take a Joint Home Loan?

Three major benefits: higher loan eligibility (combined income), doubled tax deductions, and stamp duty savings (women co-owner concession).

## Higher Loan Eligibility

| Scenario | Monthly Income | Loan Eligibility |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Single applicant | Rs 80,000 | Rs 40-48L |
| Joint (spouse earns Rs 50K) | Rs 1,30,000 | Rs 65-78L |
| **Increase** | | **60%+ higher** |

## Tax Benefits (Doubled)

Both co-owners can claim deductions individually:

| Section | Single Owner | Joint Owners (Combined) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 80C (principal) | Rs 1.5L | Rs 3L |
| 24(b) (interest, self-occupied) | Rs 2L | Rs 4L |
| **Total deduction** | **Rs 3.5L** | **Rs 7L** |
| **Tax saved (30% bracket)** | **Rs 1.09L/year** | **Rs 2.18L/year** |

Over a 20-year loan, the additional tax saving can exceed Rs 20L.

## Stamp Duty Savings

Register with wife as first-named owner:

| State | Savings (on Rs 80L property) |
| --- | --- |
| Delhi | Rs 1.6L (2% concession) |
| Haryana (Gurgaon) | Rs 1.6L |
| UP (Noida) | Rs 1.6L |
| Maharashtra | Rs 80K (1% concession) |
| Karnataka | None |

## Process for Joint Home Loan

1. Both spouses apply as co-borrowers
2. Both must submit income documents (salary slips, ITR, bank statements)
3. Both must be co-owners of the property (on sale deed)
4. Bank evaluates combined repayment capacity
5. EMI deducted from either/both accounts (flexible)

## What If One Spouse Is Not Earning?

Non-earning spouse can be co-owner but not co-borrower. Loan eligibility based only on earning spouse's income. But tax benefits and stamp duty savings still apply.

## What Happens in Case of Separation?

- Loan liability continues for both co-borrowers regardless of marital status
- Options: sell property and clear loan, one party takes over loan (requires bank approval), continue joint repayment
- Property division follows: agreement between parties, or court order
