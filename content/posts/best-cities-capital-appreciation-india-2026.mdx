## What Drives Appreciation

### Infrastructure Effect
Major infrastructure projects follow a predictable appreciation curve:

| Phase | Typical Impact | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Announcement | +5-10% | Jewar Airport announcement |
| Approval + funding | +10-15% | MTHL construction start |
| 50% construction | +15-25% | Dwarka Expressway mid-stage |
| Completion | +25-40% from base | Mumbai Metro Line 1 impact |
| Maturity (3 years) | +40-60% from base | Electronic City post-connectivity |

### Employment Growth
Every 10,000 new jobs in a micro-market drives approximately:
- 2,000-3,000 new housing demand units
- 5-8% rental rate increase
- 3-5% property price appreciation

### Supply Constraint
Markets where new launches are declining while demand grows see accelerated appreciation. South Mumbai, central Bangalore, and old Pune city fall in this category.

Don't concentrate in one city. A Rs 1 Crore portfolio could split:
- Rs 40L in Hyderabad (growth + yield)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can property prices crash in Indian cities?

Significant crashes (20%+ decline) are rare in Indian real estate due to restricted supply, high land costs, and limited speculative leverage. However, stagnation periods of 3-5 years are common in overbuilt markets. The 2015-2019 NCR slowdown is a relevant example.

### Is it too late to invest in Hyderabad?

No. While the easiest gains (2019-2023 doubling) have happened, Hyderabad's price/sqft remains 40-50% below Bangalore. With continued employment growth and infrastructure development, 8-12% annual appreciation is realistic through 2030.

### Should I prioritize appreciation over rental yield?

If you don't need rental income to cover EMI, prioritize appreciation markets. If EMI coverage matters, choose markets offering 3.5%+ yield even if appreciation is slightly lower. The ideal is both — Hyderabad and Pune currently deliver this combination.
