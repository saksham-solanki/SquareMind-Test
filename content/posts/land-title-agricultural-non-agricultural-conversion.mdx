## Why Land Conversion Matters

India's rapid urbanisation means many properties are built on land that was originally classified as agricultural. If the conversion from agricultural to non-agricultural (NA) use is incomplete or improper, the entire construction may be illegal.

## The Conversion Process

### Step 1: Apply for NA Permission

The landowner applies to the District Collector or relevant authority for change of land use from agricultural to non-agricultural (residential/commercial).

**Required documents:**
- 7/12 extract or equivalent revenue record
- Survey map
- Zone certificate from town planning authority
- No-objection from Gram Panchayat
- Soil testing report (in some states)

### Step 2: Approval and Premium Payment

If approved, the government issues an NA order and charges a premium (typically 5-15% of land value).

### Step 3: Revenue Record Update

The 7/12 extract or equivalent must be updated to reflect the new non-agricultural classification.

## State-Wise Process

| State | Authority | Key Document | Premium |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Maharashtra | District Collector | NA Order (Section 44) | 10-15% of land value |
| Karnataka | DC + Revenue Dept | DC Conversion Order | 8-12% |
| Tamil Nadu | District Collector | Land Use Change Order | 5-10% |
| Gujarat | District Collector | NA Permission | 5-8% |
| Telangana | Revenue Dept via Dharani | LUC (Land Use Certificate) | 8-10% |

## Red Flags to Watch For

1. **NA order not obtained** — construction on agricultural land is illegal
2. **Provisional NA** — final order pending; may be rejected
3. **NA for different use** — converted for industrial, selling as residential
4. **Revenue records not updated** — old records still show agricultural classification
5. **Ceiling Act violations** — land exceeding ownership ceiling may be vested in government

## What Happens If NA Is Not Obtained?

- Building is illegal and subject to demolition
- Property cannot be mortgaged (banks will not lend)
- Resale is extremely difficult
- Municipal services (water, sewage) may not be provided
- Buyer has no legal recourse

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I buy agricultural land and convert it myself?

Yes, in most states non-farmers can buy agricultural land and apply for conversion. However, some states (Karnataka, for example) restrict agricultural land purchase to farmers. Check state-specific rules.

### How long does NA conversion take?

Typically 3-12 months depending on the state and complexity. Maharashtra and Gujarat are relatively faster (3-6 months). Karnataka can take 6-12 months.
