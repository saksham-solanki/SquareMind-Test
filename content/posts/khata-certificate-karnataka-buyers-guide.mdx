## What Is a Khata?

Khata is a revenue document maintained by BBMP (Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike) or the relevant municipal body in Karnataka. It records property ownership for the purpose of property tax assessment and collection.

**Khata is not a title document.** It does not prove ownership — it only records who is paying property tax.

## A Khata vs B Khata

| Feature | A Khata | B Khata |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Definition | Property in BBMP-approved layout/plan | Property not conforming to BBMP plans |
| Building permit | Can obtain | Cannot obtain |
| Bank loan | Available | Very difficult |
| Property tax | Regular assessment | Separate assessment |
| Trade licence | Available | Not available |
| Resale value | Higher | Lower |
| Legal risk | Lower | Higher |

## How to Obtain Khata

### A Khata Application

1. Apply at the BBMP Zonal Office for your property
2. Submit: Sale deed, previous owner's khata, tax paid receipts, EC, approved plan
3. BBMP verifies and issues A Khata certificate and extract
4. Timeline: 30-60 days

### B Khata to A Khata Conversion

Possible only if the property can be regularised:
1. Apply for plan regularisation under applicable BBMP scheme
2. Pay regularisation fee and penalty
3. Once approved, apply for A Khata transfer

**Note:** Not all B Khata properties can be converted. Properties on government land, lake beds, or unauthorised zones cannot be regularised.

## Khata Certificate vs Khata Extract

| Document | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Khata Certificate | Confirms property is in municipal records under owner's name |
| Khata Extract | Detailed information — property area, zone, ward, tax assessment |

## Red Flags to Watch For

1. **B Khata property sold as A Khata** — verify at BBMP before buying
2. **No khata at all** — property may not be in municipal records (illegal construction)
3. **Khata in previous owner's name** — transfer not done after last sale
4. **Mismatch between khata area and sale deed area** — potential plan violation

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I buy a B Khata property in Bangalore?

You can, but it comes with significant restrictions — no bank loan, no building permit, and potential regularisation issues. Only consider if the price reflects these limitations (30-40% below A Khata equivalent).

### How do I check khata status online?

Visit the BBMP website (bbmp.gov.in) and search under Property Tax section using your property ID or SAS number. Online records are partially available.
