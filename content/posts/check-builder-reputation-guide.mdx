## Why Builder Verification Is Non-Negotiable

Delayed possession, quality defects, and unfulfilled promises are the top three complaints from Indian property buyers. Spending 2-3 hours on builder verification can save you years of frustration.

## Step 1: Check RERA Records

Every state RERA portal shows:
- All registered projects by the builder
- Promised vs actual completion dates
- Number of complaints filed against the builder
- Whether any project registration was revoked

| State | Portal | What to Check |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Maharashtra | maharera.mahaonline.gov.in | Promoter profile, project timeline |
| Karnataka | rera.karnataka.gov.in | Registration status, quarterly updates |
| UP | up-rera.in | Project status, buyer complaints |
| Telangana | telangana.rera.gov.in | Promoter history |
| Haryana | haryanarera.gov.in | License and registration |

## Step 2: Visit Completed Projects

This is the most valuable due diligence step. Visit the builder's previously delivered projects:

1. **Construction quality:** Look for cracks, waterproofing issues, paint quality
2. **Common area maintenance:** Clean lobbies, working lifts, maintained gardens
3. **Talk to residents:** Ask about possession delays, quality issues, builder responsiveness
4. **Check infrastructure promises:** Were the promised gym, pool, club built as shown in brochure?

## Step 3: Financial Health Check

- Check MCA (Ministry of Corporate Affairs) records for company financials
- Listed builders: Annual reports on BSE/NSE
- Any NCLT (bankruptcy) proceedings?
- Any bank NPA (non-performing asset) issues?

## Step 4: Online Research

| Source | What to Look For |
| --- | --- |
| Google Reviews | Overall sentiment, specific complaints |
| Consumer forums | Legal complaints, response quality |
| Social media | Customer grievances, builder response |
| Real estate forums | Insider opinions from existing buyers |

## Red Flags

1. **Multiple delayed projects** on RERA portal
2. **Name change:** Builder operating under a new name (check director names)
3. **No established projects:** New builder with no delivery history
4. **Aggressive pricing:** Significantly below market rate (cash flow problems)
5. **Pressure to book immediately:** "Price increase tomorrow" tactics
6. **Vague completion timeline:** No specific date on RERA

## Green Flags

1. Projects completed on or before RERA timeline
2. Happy residents in previous projects
3. Clean financial records (no NCLT, no NPA)
4. Transparent communication during construction
5. Active society handover in completed projects
