## Bangalore Property Verification: Why It Matters

Bangalore has complex land records with multiple authorities (BBMP, BDA, BMRDA). Agricultural land conversion issues and revenue site complications are common.

## Step 1: KRERA Verification

Karnataka RERA (KRERA) registration is mandatory for all projects above 8 units or 500 sqm.

**How to check:**
1. Visit [rera.karnataka.gov.in](https://rera.karnataka.gov.in)
2. Search by project name or RERA number
3. Verify: carpet area, possession date, promoter details, quarterly updates

Use our [RERA Verification Tool](/tools/rera-verifier) for guided verification.

## Step 2: Khata Verification

**Khata** is a revenue document that establishes property ownership for tax purposes. In Bangalore, there are two types:

- **A Khata** — issued by BBMP. Required for property tax payment and building permits. Properties in BBMP limits need this.
- **B Khata** — properties that don't conform to BBMP sanctioned plans. Cannot get building permit or loans easily.

**How to verify:** Apply at BBMP office or check online at bbmp.gov.in. Ensure A Khata exists before buying.

## Step 3: Encumbrance Certificate (EC)

- Available from Sub-Registrar office for the past 30 years
- Online at kaveri.karnataka.gov.in (partial records)
- Verify no mortgages, liens, or legal disputes are registered

## Step 4: Land Conversion Check

Many Bangalore outskirts properties involve agricultural-to-residential conversion.

**Verify:**
- DC (Deputy Commissioner) conversion order
- CLR (Conversion of Land Revenue) certificate
- Ensure conversion is for residential use (not industrial/commercial)

**Red flag:** Properties sold as "revenue sites" without proper BDA/BBMP plan approval.

## Step 5: BBMP/BDA Approval

- Building plan approval from BBMP (within BBMP limits) or BDA (for BDA layouts)
- Occupancy Certificate from relevant authority
- Check for plan deviations — common in Bangalore

## Red Flags to Watch For

1. **B Khata properties** — difficult to get loans, may face demolition risk
2. **Revenue sites without BDA approval** — common on outskirts, legally risky
3. **Agricultural land sold as residential** — conversion may not be complete
4. **Joint development projects** where landowner-developer disputes exist
5. **SPA (Special Power of Attorney) based sales** — verify the SPA is genuine and registered

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the difference between A Khata and B Khata in Bangalore?

A Khata means the property conforms to BBMP sanctioned plans — you can pay property tax, get building permits, and obtain bank loans. B Khata properties lack these approvals and have restricted access to loans and permits.

### How do I verify land conversion in Bangalore?

Request the DC conversion order from the seller. Verify at the Tahsildar's office. Check that the conversion is specifically for residential use and covers the exact survey number of the plot.
