## Festive Season: Genuine Deals or Marketing Hype?

Navratri-Dussehra-Diwali (September-November) is India's biggest property buying season. Builders spend crores on festive campaigns. Some deals are genuine — many are manufactured. Here's how to tell the difference.

## What Builders Actually Offer During Diwali

| Offer Type | Typical Value | Is It Real? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Price discount per sqft | Rs 200-500/sqft (3-7%) | Often genuine for unsold inventory |
| Stamp duty reimbursement | Partial or full | Usually genuine — builder absorbs cost |
| Free parking/amenities | Rs 3-8L value | Genuine if in writing |
| Modular kitchen/AC | Rs 2-5L value | Check quality — often low-spec |
| Gold coin/car on booking | Marketing gimmick | Cost embedded in price — ignore |
| "No EMI till possession" | Builder pays pre-EMI interest | Genuine but cost added to price |
| Flexi payment plan | Extended schedule | Worth evaluating |

## How to Evaluate Festive Offers

### Step 1: Get the Pre-Offer Price
Ask the sales team for the base price before festive offers. If they can't separate the two, the "offer" is likely built into an inflated base price.

### Step 2: Compare with Market Rate
Check actual transaction prices in the area (registrar data or recent RERA filings). If the "discounted" price is still above market rate, it's not a deal.

### Step 3: Get Everything in Writing
Verbal promises during site visits disappear after booking. Insist on:
- Written discount confirmation with exact per-sqft rate
- Stamp duty reimbursement clause in agreement
- Freebies listed as schedule in the agreement for sale

## Auspicious Timing vs Smart Timing

Many buyers feel compelled to register during Diwali for auspicious reasons. Smart approach:
- **Token booking during Diwali** — lock in the festive price with minimal commitment
- **Due diligence after Diwali** — take 30-45 days for proper verification
- **Registration when ready** — not when the puja muhurat says so

Your property's long-term value depends on location, builder quality, and market timing — not registration date astrology.

## Best Festive Season Strategy

1. Start research in August — shortlist projects before festive madness
2. Visit sites during Navratri — fewer crowds, same offers usually available
3. Negotiate during Diwali week — maximum builder pressure
4. Don't sign on the spot — take the offer letter home
5. Complete due diligence before final payment

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are Diwali property prices actually lower?

On average, genuine festive discounts are 3-7% below non-festive pricing. However, some builders inflate prices in September and then "discount" to normal levels in October. Always benchmark against 6-month price history.

### Is it bad luck to buy property during certain months?

This is a cultural belief, not a financial principle. Statistically, properties purchased in any month perform identically if location, builder, and pricing fundamentals are sound.

### Should I wait for Diwali offers or buy when I find a good deal?

If you find a genuinely good deal (below market rate, verified builder, right location) before Diwali, take it. Waiting 2-3 months for a potential 3-5% discount risks price appreciation wiping out those savings.
