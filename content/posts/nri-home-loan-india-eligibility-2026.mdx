## NRI Home Loan: What Indian Banks Actually Offer in 2026

NRI home loans come with different terms than resident Indian home loans. Higher interest rates, lower LTV ratios, and significantly more documentation. Here is what to expect.

## Eligibility Criteria

| Criteria | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Age | 21-60 years (at loan maturity) |
| Minimum income | Varies (typically USD 3,000+/month or equivalent) |
| Employment | Salaried or self-employed (2+ years in current role) |
| Country of residence | Most countries accepted (some sanctions-list exclusions) |
| CIBIL score | 700+ preferred |
| Property type | Residential or commercial (under construction or ready) |

## Documentation Required

### From Abroad
- Valid Indian passport and visa/PR of current country
- Employment letter or contract
- Last 6 months salary slips
- Last 2 years overseas tax returns
- Last 6 months bank statements (overseas account)
- NRE/NRO account statements

### For the Property
- Property documents (agreement, title deed, approved plan)
- Builder/seller KYC documents
- RERA registration certificate
- Property valuation report

## Processing Timeline

Expect 3-8 weeks from application to disbursement. Start your loan application before finalizing the property — not after. Pre-approval gives you negotiating power and speed advantage.

## Repayment Options

EMI must be paid from NRE or NRO account. Some banks accept direct foreign remittance for EMI payment. Standing instructions can be set up for automatic debit.
