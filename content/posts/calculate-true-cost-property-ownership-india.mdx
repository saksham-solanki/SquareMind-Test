## The ₹1 Crore Flat Actually Costs ₹1.65 Crore — Here's the Math

When you buy a ₹1 crore property in India, the actual cost over 10 years of ownership is ₹1.5-1.8 crore. The gap between sticker price and true cost is where most investors miscalculate their returns and cash flow requirements.

## Complete Cost Breakdown for ₹1 Crore Property

### Acquisition Costs (Day 0)

| Component | Rate | Amount | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Property price | - | ₹1,00,00,000 | Base price |
| Stamp duty | 5-7% (state dependent) | ₹5,00,000-7,00,000 | Varies by state |
| Registration | 1% | ₹1,00,000 | Capped in some states |
| GST (under-construction) | 5% / 1% | ₹1,00,000-5,00,000 | Zero for ready |
| Legal/documentation | Flat fee | ₹25,000-50,000 | Title search, agreement |
| Brokerage | 1-2% | ₹1,00,000-2,00,000 | If broker involved |
| **Total acquisition** | | **₹1,08,25,000-1,15,50,000** | **8-15% above property price** |

### Financing Costs (Over Loan Tenure)

| Component | Monthly | Over 20 Years |
| --- | --- | --- |
| EMI | ₹69,400 | ₹1,66,56,000 |
| Total interest paid | - | ₹86,56,000 |

### Exit Costs

| Component | Rate | On ₹1.5Cr sale |
| --- | --- | --- |

| Brokerage (selling) | 1% | ₹1,50,000 |
| Prepayment penalty (if applicable) | 0-2% of outstanding | ₹0-80,000 |

## True Cost Summary (10-Year Ownership)

| Category | Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Acquisition costs | ₹1,10,00,000 |
| Interest (10 years of 20-year loan) | ₹58,00,000 |
| Annual costs (10 years) | ₹12,00,000 |
| Exit costs | ₹6,00,000 |
| **Total true cost** | **₹1,86,00,000** |

Against this ₹1.86Cr total cost, if the property appreciates to ₹2.6Cr (10% CAGR over 10 years) and generates ₹6L in net rental income, your net profit is ₹80L — a 43% return on total cost, or about 8.2% annualised on true cost.

## The Bottom Line

The true cost of property ownership in India is 50-85% above the sticker price over a 10-year ownership period (including interest, taxes, and maintenance). Factor this into every ROI calculation. A property that "appreciates 100%" over 10 years may only deliver 8-10% annualised return after all costs.
