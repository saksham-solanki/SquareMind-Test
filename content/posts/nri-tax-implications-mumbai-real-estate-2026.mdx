## Mumbai Property Tax for NRIs: What You Must Know

Mumbai remains the top destination for NRI property investment in India. But the tax implications are significantly different from resident Indian buyers. Getting this wrong can cost you 20-30% of your transaction value in avoidable taxes.

## TDS on Property Purchase and Sale

### When Buying
The buyer must deduct TDS at 1% of the sale consideration if the property value exceeds Rs 50 lakhs (Section 194-IA). This applies whether the seller is NRI or resident.

### When Selling (NRI Seller)
This is where NRIs face the biggest surprise. When an NRI sells property, the buyer must deduct TDS at:

| Holding Period | TDS Rate | Tax Type |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Less than 2 years | 30% + surcharge + cess | Short-term capital gains |
| More than 2 years | 20% + surcharge + cess | Long-term capital gains |

**Effective TDS rate can reach 30-34%** of the sale amount — not just the profit. Many NRIs discover this only at registration.

## Capital Gains Tax Structure

| Type | Holding Period | Tax Rate | Indexation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Short-term | Less than 2 years | 30% (slab rate) | No |
| Long-term | 2+ years | 20% | Yes (CII indexed) |

### Section 54 Exemption
Reinvest long-term capital gains in another residential property within 2 years (purchase) or 3 years (construction) to claim full exemption. The new property must be in India.

### Section 54EC Bonds
Invest up to Rs 50 lakhs in specified bonds (NHAI, REC) within 6 months of sale to claim exemption. Lock-in period is 5 years.

## Rental Income Taxation

Mumbai rental income for NRIs is taxed at slab rates after standard 30% deduction:

| Annual Rental Income | Tax After 30% Deduction | Effective Rate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Rs 5,00,000 | Rs 3,50,000 taxable | ~5% |
| Rs 10,00,000 | Rs 7,00,000 taxable | ~15% |
| Rs 20,00,000 | Rs 14,00,000 taxable | ~25% |

Property management companies in Mumbai typically charge 8-12% of rental income for NRI landlords.

## DTAA Benefits by Country

| Country | DTAA Benefit | Key Provision |
| --- | --- | --- |
| USA | Tax credit | India tax offset against US federal tax |
| UK | Tax credit | India tax offset via Self Assessment |
| Canada | Tax credit | India tax deducted from Canadian tax |
| UAE | No income tax | Only India tax applies |
| Singapore | Tax credit | India tax offset against SG tax |

NRIs from the US must also report Indian property on FBAR (FinCEN 114) if total foreign accounts exceed $10,000 at any point during the year.

## Repatriation Rules

- Sale proceeds from up to 2 residential properties can be repatriated
- Maximum repatriation: USD 1 million per financial year under Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS)
- Requires Form 15CA/15CB certification from a Chartered Accountant
- Bank processes through NRO account to foreign account

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I avoid the 30% TDS when selling Mumbai property?

You can apply for a lower TDS certificate from the Income Tax department (Form 13). This requires filing projected capital gains computation showing actual tax liability, which is usually lower than 30% of sale value.

### Is stamp duty different for NRIs in Mumbai?

No. NRIs pay the same stamp duty as residents in Maharashtra. Currently 5% for men and 4% for women in Mumbai and surrounding areas. Check rates with the [Stamp Duty Calculator](/tools/stamp-duty-calculator).

### Should I buy Mumbai property in joint name with resident spouse?

Joint ownership with a resident Indian can simplify property management and rental collection. However, the NRI's share of capital gains remains taxable under NRI provisions regardless of joint ownership.
