## The Township Pioneer

Hiranandani is synonymous with Powai — they transformed a hilly, inaccessible area of Mumbai into one of the city's most desirable addresses. Can they replicate this success elsewhere?

## Company Overview

- **Private company** (Hiranandani Group / House of Hiranandani)
- **Headquarters:** Mumbai
- **Founded:** 1978 by Niranjan and Surendra Hiranandani
- **Active cities:** Mumbai (Powai, Thane), Bangalore (Devanahalli, Hebbal), Chennai
- **Model:** Large township developments with integrated infrastructure
- **Note:** Group has split — House of Hiranandani (Bangalore/Chennai) operates separately from Hiranandani Group (Mumbai)

## RERA Delivery Track Record

Analysis of 15 Hiranandani projects across MahaRERA and Karnataka RERA (2017-2023).

| Category | Projects | % of Total |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Delivered on time | 7 | 46.7% |
| Delayed 6–18 months | 5 | 33.3% |
| Delayed 18–36 months | 2 | 13.3% |
| Significantly delayed | 1 | 6.7% |

**Assessment:** 46.7% on-time delivery is above average. The township model, once land acquisition and approvals are sorted, enables decent execution. Powai projects have the best track record.

## The Township Model

Hiranandani's strength is the township model — self-contained communities with roads, schools, hospitals, retail, and parks. This provides:

- **Lifestyle advantage** — living in a Hiranandani township is materially different from a standalone project
- **Appreciation pattern** — township properties appreciate as infrastructure matures
- **Maintenance quality** — centrally managed amenities and common areas

## Family Split: What Buyers Should Know

The Hiranandani family has split the business. Niranjan Hiranandani manages Mumbai operations. The House of Hiranandani brand manages Bangalore and Chennai. **These are now separate entities — do not assume Mumbai track record applies to Bangalore or vice versa.**

## Our Verdict

**Overall Grade: B+**

Hiranandani's township model creates genuinely superior living environments. Powai remains a testament to long-term township value. The family split creates some brand confusion. Stick to established townships for best results.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Hiranandani Powai still a good investment?

Hiranandani Powai is a mature, well-established township. Appreciation will be moderate (it's already premium-priced) but rental yields and lifestyle quality are excellent. Good for end-use.

### Is House of Hiranandani Bangalore the same company?

No. The Hiranandani family has split operations. House of Hiranandani (Bangalore, Chennai) operates independently from Hiranandani Group (Mumbai). Evaluate each separately.
