## What Is RERA?

The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 is the most important buyer protection law in Indian real estate. It mandates registration of all residential projects above 500 sq mt or 8 apartments, regulates builder practices, and provides a complaint mechanism for buyers.

## Key Buyer Protections Under RERA

### 1. Project Registration

Every project must be registered with the state RERA authority before advertising or selling. Registration requires:

- Approved building plan from local authority
- Legal title of the land
- Project timeline with completion date
- 70% of buyer payments deposited in a designated escrow account

### 2. Carpet Area Standardization

RERA mandates that property is sold based on carpet area only (not super built-up area). This prevents the common practice of inflating area by including walls, common spaces, and other non-usable areas.

### 3. Builder Obligations

| Obligation | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Completion deadline | Must deliver within the registered timeline |
| Quality warranty | 5-year structural defect liability |
| Escrow account | 70% of collections in separate bank account |
| No plan changes | Cannot modify approved plan without 2/3 buyer consent |
| Information disclosure | All project details on RERA website |

### 4. Buyer Rights

| Right | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Refund on delay | Full refund with interest if builder delays |
| Compensation | Interest for delayed possession |
| Quality | Defect rectification within 30 days or compensation |
| Information | Access to project documents and approvals |
| Transfer | Right to form residents' association |

## How to File a RERA Complaint

1. Register on your state's RERA portal
2. File complaint online with project details and issue description
3. Pay nominal filing fee (Rs 1,000-5,000 depending on state)
4. RERA authority issues notice to builder
5. Hearing scheduled within 60 days
6. Order issued — typically within 90 days of filing

## Limitations of RERA

- Projects launched before RERA may have limited coverage
- Plotted developments (in some states) may be exempt
- Enforcement varies significantly by state
- Penalty collection from builders can be slow

## Before Buying: RERA Verification Checklist

1. Verify project RERA registration number on state portal
2. Check registered completion date
3. Review builder's past project completion record on RERA
4. Check for pending complaints against the builder
5. Verify carpet area matches RERA registered specification
