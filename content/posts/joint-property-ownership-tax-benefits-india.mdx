## Why Joint Ownership Makes Tax Sense

Joint property ownership with a spouse or family member can save you significant tax through stamp duty concessions, doubled home loan deductions, split rental income, and individual capital gains exemptions.

## Stamp Duty Savings

| State | Male Buyer | Female First-Named | Savings (on Rs 1Cr) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Maharashtra | 6% | 5% | Rs 1L |
| Delhi | 6% | 4% | Rs 2L |
| Haryana | 7% | 5% | Rs 2L |
| UP | 7% | 5% | Rs 2L |
| Karnataka | 5% | 5% | None |

## Home Loan Tax Deductions (Doubled)

Both co-owners can claim individual tax deductions on a joint home loan:

| Deduction | Per Owner | Combined (2 owners) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Section 80C (principal) | Rs 1.5L | Rs 3L |
| Section 24(b) (interest) | Rs 2L | Rs 4L |
| **Total annual deduction** | **Rs 3.5L** | **Rs 7L** |

**Tax saving (at 30% bracket):** Rs 2.1L per year (combined) vs Rs 1.05L for single owner.

## Rental Income Splitting

If co-owned property generates rental income, each owner reports only their share:

| Share Pattern | Owner 1 Income | Owner 2 Income | Tax Advantage |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 50-50 | Rs 3L | Rs 3L | Each in lower bracket |
| 60-40 | Rs 3.6L | Rs 2.4L | Lower earner benefits more |

If one co-owner has no other income, their rental income may fall entirely within the tax-free limit (Rs 3L for individuals under 60).

## Capital Gains Split

On sale, capital gains are split proportionally. Each owner can independently claim Section 54 exemption — effectively doubling the tax-free reinvestment limit.

## Requirements for Valid Joint Ownership

1. Both names must appear on the sale deed
2. Share ratio should be clearly stated
3. Each co-owner must have their own PAN
4. Joint home loan requires both to be co-borrowers
5. Payment contributions should be documented
