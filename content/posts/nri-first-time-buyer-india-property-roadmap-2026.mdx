## Your Complete NRI Property Purchase Playbook

Buying property in India as an NRI involves navigating two regulatory frameworks — FEMA (foreign exchange) and Indian real estate law. Most NRIs spend months figuring this out through trial and error. This roadmap gives you the exact sequence of steps.

## Phase 1: Financial Setup (4–6 Weeks Before Purchase)

### Step 1: Open NRO Account (If Not Already Open)

Your property transaction funds flow through NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) account. While you can also use NRE for inward remittance, the actual property registration and TDS handling happens through NRO.

### Step 2: Arrange Home Loan (Optional)

NRI home loans are available from SBI, HDFC, ICICI, and most major banks. Key differences from resident loans:
- Interest rates: 0.25–0.5% higher than resident rates
- LTV ratio: Usually 75–80% (vs 80–90% for residents)
- Repayment: Through NRE/NRO account or overseas bank
- Processing time: 4–6 weeks (longer than resident processing)

### Step 3: Arrange Power of Attorney

If you can't be physically present for registration, execute a POA for a trusted person in India:
- **General POA:** Broad authority (use cautiously)
- **Specific POA:** Recommended — limited to the specific transaction
- POA must be attested by Indian consulate/embassy or notarized and apostilled

Never skip this. Check project registration status using our [RERA Verifier](/tools/rera-verifier) tool.

### Step 6: Builder Due Diligence

Verify the builder's delivery track record, financial health, and legal standing. Physical site visits (or video calls with trusted contacts) are essential.

## Phase 3: Purchase Execution (2–4 Weeks)

### Step 7: Negotiate and Sign Agreement

- Agreement for Sale (AFS) must include RERA registration number
- Payment schedule should align with construction milestones (for under-construction)
- Ensure cancellation and refund clauses are clear

### Step 9: Post-Registration

- Collect registered sale deed (original)
- Apply for property mutation at municipal office
- Start home loan EMI (if applicable)
- Insure the property

## Phase 4: Ongoing Management

### Tax Compliance

File Indian income tax returns annually declaring rental income (if rented) or deemed rent (if vacant). Use our [NRI Tax Calculator](/tools/nri-tax-calculator) to estimate liability.

### Repatriation Planning

When you eventually sell, proceeds can be repatriated through NRO account subject to TDS deduction and CA certificate.

## FAQs

### Can NRIs buy agricultural land in India?

No — FEMA prohibits NRIs from purchasing agricultural land, plantation property, or farmhouses. Only residential and commercial properties are allowed.

### Do I need to visit India for property registration?

Not if you have a valid POA. But we recommend at least one visit for site inspection before committing.

### Can I buy property jointly with a resident Indian?

Yes — joint ownership with a resident Indian (spouse, parent, sibling) is common and can offer tax advantages.
