## The Yield Gap: Why Commercial Deserves Your Attention

Commercial real estate in India yields 6-9% gross, compared to 2-4% for residential. Yet most Indian investors default to residential because it's familiar. This yield gap represents a significant opportunity for investors with ₹1Cr+ budgets who are willing to learn a different asset class.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

| Factor | Residential | Commercial |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Gross rental yield | 2-4% | 6-9% |
| Capital appreciation | 8-16% CAGR | 5-10% CAGR |
| Minimum investment | ₹25L-50L | ₹80L-2Cr |
| Tenant lease duration | 11 months (renewable) | 3-9 years (lock-in) |
| Vacancy risk | LOW (individuals always need homes) | MEDIUM (corporate cycles) |
| Management complexity | LOW | MEDIUM |
| Loan availability | 80% LTV, lower rates | 60-70% LTV, higher rates |
| Liquidity | HIGH (faster resale) | MEDIUM (fewer buyers) |
| GST on rent | NIL | 18% (passed to tenant) |
| Depreciation benefit | No | Yes (for tax) |

## When Commercial Wins

### Pure Income Plays
If your primary goal is monthly income, commercial real estate at 6-9% yield generates 2-3x the income of residential at equivalent capital. A ₹1.5Cr office in Navi Mumbai (Airoli/Vashi) yields ₹8,000-10,000/month per lakh invested, versus ₹3,000-4,000 for residential.

### Long-Term Tenants
Commercial leases with 3-9 year lock-in periods provide income predictability that residential can't match. Annual escalation clauses (typically 5-8%) compound returns.

## When Residential Wins

### Capital Appreciation
Residential properties in growth corridors appreciate faster (10-16% CAGR) than commercial (5-10%). If capital gains matter more than rental income, residential wins.

### Liquidity and Exit
Residential properties sell faster — 2-4 months in established markets versus 6-12 months for commercial. This matters when you need to exit.

### Lower Entry Point
₹30-50L gets you a quality residential investment. Commercial requires ₹80L+ for a viable unit.

## REITs: The Accessible Alternative

For investors who want commercial real estate exposure below ₹80L, REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts) offer a solution. Embassy REIT, Mindspace REIT, and Brookfield REIT trade on BSE/NSE, invest in premium office spaces, and distribute 90%+ of rental income as dividends.

**Typical REIT yield:** 6-7% annually + 3-5% NAV appreciation.

## The Bottom Line

Commercial real estate deserves a place in every serious investor's portfolio — the yield advantage is too significant to ignore. Residential for appreciation + liquidity, commercial for income + stability. The ideal portfolio combines both, weighted by your income vs growth priority.
