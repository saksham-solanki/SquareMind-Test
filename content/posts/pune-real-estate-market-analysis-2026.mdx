## Pune's Real Estate Market: Steady Growth Backed by Fundamentals

Pune recorded 1.08 lakh property registrations in 2025, an 11% increase over 2024. Unlike Mumbai's bifurcated market, Pune's growth is distributed more evenly across corridors — western Pune (Hinjewadi belt), eastern Pune (Kharadi–Wagholi), and central Pune (Baner–Aundh) all grew between 9–15%.

This broad-based growth reflects Pune's diversified economy: IT services, automotive (Tata, Bajaj, Mercedes manufacturing), education (50+ colleges), and the military establishment all contribute to housing demand.

## Supply and Demand Analysis

### New Launches
Pune saw 72,000 new residential units launched in 2025. The western corridor (Hinjewadi–Wakad–Tathawade) accounted for 40% of volume, with Godrej, Kolte-Patil, and VTP Realty as the most active launchers.

### Absorption
Overall absorption rate: 68–72%, among the healthiest in India. Kharadi leads at 75%+ absorption, reflecting strong demand from eastern IT corridor employment.

### Price-to-Income Ratio
Pune's price-to-income ratio (property price / annual household income) is 4.5–6x for IT professionals, compared to 8–10x in Mumbai and 6–8x in Bangalore. This affordability supports continued demand.

## Demand Drivers

### IT and GCC Expansion
Hinjewadi IT Park is India's largest IT park by employee count. Rajiv Gandhi IT Park in Kharadi and Magarpatta Cybercity add eastern corridor demand. GCC growth has been 15% annually since 2022.

### Automotive and Manufacturing
Chakan, Talegaon, and Ranjangaon industrial belts employ 2 lakh+ workers who drive affordable housing demand in the ₹25–45L segment.

### Education and Defence
Pune's 50+ engineering and management colleges and the Southern Command headquarters create consistent demand for rental housing.

Metro Line 3 (Hinjewadi–Shivajinagar) will be the biggest catalyst for western Pune property prices when construction begins. Early positioning near planned stations offers 25–35% appreciation potential.

## Predictions for 2026–2028

**Base case (55%):** IT growth continues at 10–12%, infrastructure progresses, no major policy changes. Growth corridors appreciate 10–14% annually.

**Bull case (25%):** Metro Line 3 approved and begins construction, Mumbai–Pune Hyperloop gets timeline, new GCC announcements. Western Pune sees 15–20% appreciation.

**Bear case (20%):** IT hiring slowdown, oversupply in western corridor, infrastructure delays. Appreciation moderates to 5–8%.

## The Bottom Line

Pune's real estate market is one of India's most fundamentally sound — affordable pricing, strong employment growth, healthy absorption rates, and improving infrastructure. The western corridor (Hinjewadi Phase 3, Tathawade, Kiwale) offers the best appreciation potential, while eastern Pune (Kharadi, Wagholi) provides a balanced yield-appreciation profile.
