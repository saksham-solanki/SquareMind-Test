## Two Growth Corridors, Different Timelines

North Bangalore (Devanahalli-Hebbal-Yelahanka) and South Bangalore (Electronic City-Sarjapur-Kanakapura Road) compete as Bangalore's primary investment corridors. The choice depends on whether you prioritize infrastructure-led growth or established IT employment.

## Price and Growth Comparison

| Parameter | North Bangalore | South Bangalore |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Average price/sqft | Rs 5,500-10,000 | Rs 6,000-12,000 |
| Premium areas | Rs 8,000-14,000 (Hebbal) | Rs 10,000-18,000 (Sarjapur Road) |
| Growth zones | Rs 4,000-7,000 (Devanahalli) | Rs 5,000-8,000 (Kanakapura Road) |
| YoY appreciation | 12-18% | 8-12% |
| Rental yield | 3.0-4.0% | 3.8-5.0% |

North Bangalore shows higher appreciation rates driven by airport proximity and infrastructure development. South Bangalore offers better immediate rental yields from established IT employment.

## North Bangalore: Airport-Led Growth

- **Kempegowda International Airport** — India's busiest airport expansion (Terminal 2 operational)
- **Aerospace SEZ** — HAL, Boeing, Airbus presence creating employment
- **KIAL IT Investment Region** — proposed 12,000-acre IT zone
- **Devanahalli Business Park** — integrated business district
- **Bellary Road widening** — improving connectivity to city center
- **Metro extension** — proposed airport metro link

### Best Micro-Markets
- Devanahalli (Rs 4,500-7,000) — closest to airport, maximum upside
- Yelahanka (Rs 6,000-9,000) — better connectivity, established residential
- Hebbal (Rs 8,000-14,000) — premium, well-connected, immediate rental demand

## South Bangalore: IT Employment Hub

- **Electronic City Phase 1 & 2** — Infosys, Wipro, TCS mega campuses
- **Sarjapur Road** — fastest-growing IT corridor
- **NICE Road connectivity** — ring road access
- **Established social infrastructure** — schools, hospitals, retail
- **Metro Phase 2** — connecting Electronic City to CBD

### Best Micro-Markets
- Sarjapur Road (Rs 7,000-12,000) — strong IT rental demand
- Electronic City Phase 1 (Rs 5,500-8,000) — campus adjacency premium
- HSR Layout (Rs 10,000-16,000) — startup hub, walkable amenities
- Kanakapura Road (Rs 5,000-8,000) — affordable with metro connectivity

## Investment Recommendation

**North Bangalore:** Best for 5-7 year capital appreciation plays. Buy near airport/aerospace corridor and hold. Lower current rental income but higher upside potential.

**South Bangalore:** Best for rental income strategy. Immediate IT tenant demand. Lower appreciation ceiling but more predictable returns.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is North Bangalore overrated?

Some segments are overhyped. Devanahalli plots beyond 15km from the airport have limited near-term catalysts. Stick to areas within 10km of airport or along the Bellary Road corridor for genuine infrastructure-led appreciation.

### Will the airport metro change North Bangalore pricing?

Yes significantly. Airport metro connectivity will reduce CBD commute time from 90+ minutes to under 40 minutes. This alone could add 20-30% to prices in connected stations like Hebbal, Yelahanka, and Nagawara.

### Which corridor has better water availability?

North Bangalore has better access to Cauvery water supply (Hebbal, Yelahanka). Parts of South Bangalore (outer Sarjapur, Electronic City) rely heavily on borewells. Water availability is increasingly a price-determining factor.
