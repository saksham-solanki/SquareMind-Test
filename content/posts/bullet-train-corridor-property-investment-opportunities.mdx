## India's First Bullet Train: Real Estate Along 508 km

The Mumbai-Ahmedabad High Speed Rail (MAHSR) corridor is India's first bullet train project. With 12 stations across 508 km, this infrastructure project will reshape real estate markets in western India.

## Timing Considerations

The project timeline has faced delays. Current expected completion: 2028-2030. The real estate impact curve:

1. **Now (announcement phase):** 10-15% price appreciation already captured
2. **Construction visible (2025-26):** Another 10-15% as confidence builds
3. **Trial runs (2028):** 10-15% final push
4. **Operational (2029-30):** Prices stabilize at new equilibrium

**Best entry point:** Now through 2026, before construction-phase appreciation kicks in fully.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Will the bullet train actually be completed on time?

The project has already faced 3+ years of delays from land acquisition issues. 2028-2030 completion is the current realistic estimate. Budget for a 2-3 year delay in your investment timeline.

### Is it worth investing in small station towns like Bilimora?

Only if you have a 7-10 year investment horizon and accept that the market is illiquid today. The upside potential is significant (30-40%) but the risk of further delays and limited resale options is real.

### How does bullet train compare to metro for property impact?

Metro has more direct daily-commute impact on residential prices. Bullet train primarily benefits inter-city travel and commercial activity. The property impact is broader but less intense than metro station proximity.
