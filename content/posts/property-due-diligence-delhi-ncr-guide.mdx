## NCR: India's Highest-Risk Property Market

Delhi NCR has the highest rate of project delays, builder defaults, and property fraud in India. Due diligence is not optional — it is critical.

## The Regulatory Maze

NCR spans three states with different RERA authorities:

| Location | RERA Authority | Website |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Gurgaon/Haryana | HRERA (Haryana RERA) | haryanarera.gov.in |
| Noida/Greater Noida | UP RERA | up-rera.in |
| Delhi | Delhi RERA | rera.delhi.gov.in |

## Step 1: RERA Verification (Critical)

In NCR, RERA verification is the most important step. Many projects have been delayed or abandoned.

**Check:**
- Registration status (active, expired, revoked?)
- Registered possession date vs marketed date
- Number of complaints filed
- Quarterly progress reports (is construction progressing?)
- Builder's other projects and their completion status

## Step 2: Title and Ownership

**Gurgaon:** Check with Tehsildar office. Verify sale deed chain through Sadar sub-registrar office. Many Gurgaon properties are on licence-based land from Haryana government — verify the licence terms.

**Noida:** Leasehold properties from Noida Authority/Greater Noida Authority. Verify allotment letter, lease deed, and authority dues clearance.

**Delhi:** Freehold properties — verify through Revenue Department. DDA properties — verify allotment and conversion status.

## Step 3: Builder Financial Health Check

**Critical in NCR.** Multiple NCR builders have defaulted (Unitech, Jaypee, Amrapali). Before buying:

- Check if builder is listed (financial data available)
- Search NCLT (National Company Law Tribunal) for insolvency proceedings
- Check consumer forum cases on confonet.nic.in
- Verify builder's bank account has funds (RERA escrow account)

## Step 4: Authority Approvals

| Document | Authority | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Layout plan approval | DTCP (Haryana) / Authority (Noida) | Land use permission |
| Building plan approval | DTCP / Noida Authority | Construction permission |
| Environmental clearance | MOEF / SEIAA | For large projects |
| Fire NOC | Fire department | Safety clearance |
| Occupation certificate | Municipal authority | Habitation permission |

## Red Flags to Watch For

1. **Builder under NCLT proceedings** — check before paying any amount
2. **Subvention schemes** — builder pays EMI during construction; if builder defaults, buyer still owes bank
3. **Registry not done despite possession** — common in Noida; creates legal limbo
4. **Authority dues unpaid** — buyer may inherit pending dues to Noida/Greater Noida Authority
5. **Change of land use not approved** — some projects built on land designated for other purposes

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Noida property safe to buy?

Noida has had significant issues with builder defaults (Amrapali, Jaypee). Stick to established developers — DLF, Tata, Godrej, ATS — in Noida. Verify RERA and builder financial health thoroughly.

### What is the difference between freehold and leasehold in NCR?

Delhi offers freehold ownership. Gurgaon is freehold on private land, licence-based on government land. Noida is leasehold — you lease land from Noida Authority for 90-99 years. Leasehold has renewal implications.
