## Noida's Market Recovery: From Crisis to Cautious Optimism

Noida's real estate market spent 2018-2022 in crisis mode — builder insolvencies, stalled projects, and buyer trust at rock bottom. The recovery since 2023 has been remarkable: RERA enforcement improved, stressed projects resumed under IBC resolution, national developers entered, and Jewar Airport construction began.

Property registrations in Noida and Greater Noida grew 22% in 2025, the fastest among NCR sub-markets.

## Supply Dynamics

### Delivered vs Stalled Projects
As of 2026, approximately 1.5 lakh units in Noida and Greater Noida are in various stages of delayed delivery. RERA UP has resolved about 40% of these, with remaining projects under timeline monitoring. The overhang of undelivered projects continues to suppress new launch absorption in some sectors.

### National Developer Entry
Godrej Properties, Tata Housing, Birla Estates, and Max Estates have entered or expanded in Noida, raising the quality baseline. These developers are selectively buying land in premium sectors (128-150), avoiding the distressed areas.

## Predictions for 2026-2028

**Base case (45%):** Jewar construction progresses, builder recovery continues, RERA enforcement maintains. Noida Expressway 10-14%, Greater Noida West 12-16%.

**Bull case (25%):** Jewar opens on schedule (2027), metro extension approved, new IT park announced. 16-22% across connected corridors.

**Bear case (30%):** Airport delays extend to 2029, builder defaults resume, UP policy uncertainty. 3-7% appreciation, some pockets flat.

The bear case probability is higher for Noida than other markets because of the builder trust legacy.

## The Bottom Line

Noida is NCR's most asymmetric bet — the downside is known (builder risk, delays), but the upside (Jewar Airport) is potentially transformative. For investors who can do thorough due diligence and have 5+ year horizons, Noida offers NCR's best appreciation potential. For risk-averse investors, Gurgaon remains the safer choice.
